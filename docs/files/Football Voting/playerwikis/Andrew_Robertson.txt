<p><b>Andrew Henry Robertson</b> (born 11 March 1994) is a Scottish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland national team</a>, which he captains. Robertson began his senior career with <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Park_F.C." title="Queen&#39;s Park F.C.">Queen's Park</a> in 2012 before joining <a href="/wiki/Dundee_United_F.C." title="Dundee United F.C.">Dundee United</a> a year later. His form in his first season as a professional led to him being named <a href="/wiki/PFA_Scotland_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Scotland Young Player of the Year">PFA Scotland Young Player of the Year</a> and also making his international debut. He joined <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in July 2014 for a fee of £2.85&#160;million, before joining Liverpool in July 2017 for an undisclosed fee, believed to be an initial £8&#160;million. He appeared in successive <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_Cup_and_UEFA_Champions_League_finals" title="List of European Cup and UEFA Champions League finals">UEFA Champions League finals</a> in his first two seasons, losing the first in <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2018 UEFA Champions League Final">2018</a>. For his performances in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Premier_League" title="2018–19 Premier League">2018–19 Premier League</a> season, he was named in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year_(2010s)#Premier_League_10" title="PFA Team of the Year (2010s)">PFA Team of the Year</a>. His third season saw him play an integral role as Liverpool won the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Premier_League" title="2019–20 Premier League">2019–20 Premier League</a>.
</p><p>Robertson made his full international debut for Scotland in May 2014, and he was appointed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scotland_national_football_team_captains" title="List of Scotland national football team captains">Scotland captain</a> in September 2018.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robertson was born in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and was a childhood <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> fan.<sup id="cite_ref-places_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His father Brian, nicknamed 'Pop', who grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Maryhill" title="Maryhill">Maryhill</a> area (specifically the <a href="/wiki/Wyndford" title="Wyndford">Wyndford</a> estate where he was a childhood friend of footballers <a href="/wiki/Jim_Duffy_(footballer)" title="Jim Duffy (footballer)">Jim Duffy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Nicholas" title="Charlie Nicholas">Charlie Nicholas</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-places_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was a keen amateur player, although a spinal injury requiring him to wear a back brace prevented him from pursuing sport as a career.<sup id="cite_ref-places_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/St_Ninian%27s_High_School,_Giffnock" title="St Ninian&#39;s High School, Giffnock">St Ninian's High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Giffnock" title="Giffnock">Giffnock</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Renfrewshire" title="East Renfrewshire">East Renfrewshire</a>, from 2006 to 2012, where he captained the football team. He also played for Giffnock Soccer Centre and joined Celtic as a youth, but was released at under-15 level because he was considered to be too small.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He then signed for <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Park_F.C." title="Queen&#39;s Park F.C.">Queen's Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-places_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Queen.27s_Park"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen's_Park">Queen's Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queen&#039;s Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Focusing on his studies, Robertson was on the brink of starting university at undergraduate degree level before he was called up to the Queen's Park first team at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_in_Scottish_football" title="2012–13 in Scottish football">2012–13 season</a>. He made his debut for the club in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Challenge_Cup" title="Scottish Challenge Cup">Scottish Challenge Cup</a> in a penalty shoot out win against <a href="/wiki/Berwick_Rangers_F.C." title="Berwick Rangers F.C.">Berwick Rangers</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and went on to make a total of forty appearances during the season,<sup id="cite_ref-Andrew_Robertson_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andrew_Robertson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> helping the club to a third-place finish in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Scottish_Third_Division" title="2012–13 Scottish Third Division">2012–13 Scottish Third Division</a>. His first goal for the club came in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/East_Stirlingshire_F.C." title="East Stirlingshire F.C.">East Stirlingshire</a> on 13 November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Andrew_Robertson_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andrew_Robertson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dundee_United">Dundee United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dundee United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Robertson signed for <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premiership" title="Scottish Premiership">Scottish Premiership</a> side <a href="/wiki/Dundee_United_F.C." title="Dundee United F.C.">Dundee United</a>, along with Queen's Park teammate <a href="/wiki/Aidan_Connolly" title="Aidan Connolly">Aidan Connolly</a>, on 3 June 2013;<sup id="cite_ref-Dundee_United_sign_Robertson_and_Connolly_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dundee_United_sign_Robertson_and_Connolly-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> he took the squad place of the departing <a href="/wiki/Barry_Douglas" title="Barry Douglas">Barry Douglas</a> who had made the same move three years earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Having been ignored in their requests for a transfer fee for Douglas, Queen's Park (an amateur club) protested at the prospect of more players departing to United for nothing and eventually agreed a deal involving a percentage of future transfers, which would later prove beneficial.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robertson immediately became part of manager <a href="/wiki/Jackie_McNamara" title="Jackie McNamara">Jackie McNamara</a>'s first team plans, making his debut for the club on the opening day of the season in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Partick_Thistle_F.C." title="Partick Thistle F.C.">Partick Thistle</a>. On 22 September 2013, Robertson scored his first goal for Dundee United, in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Motherwell_F.C." title="Motherwell F.C.">Motherwell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He ran from his own half before sending a low left-foot drive in to the goal from 22 yards.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Soon afterwards he agreed a new contract with United, until May 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He was voted <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Professional_Football_League_monthly_awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Professional Football League monthly awards">SPFL young player of the month</a> for September 2013 and player of the month for November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-pom1113_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pom1113-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 12 April 2014, Robertson played in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Scottish_Cup" title="2013–14 Scottish Cup">Scottish Cup</a> Semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ibrox_Stadium" title="Ibrox Stadium">Ibrox</a>, which United won 3–1.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In April 2014, Robertson won the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Scotland_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Scotland Young Player of the Year">PFA Scotland Young Player of the Year</a> award<sup id="cite_ref-ypoy14_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ypoy14-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and was also named in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Scotland_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Scotland Team of the Year">PFA Scotland Team of the Year</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Scottish_Premiership" title="2013–14 Scottish Premiership">2013–14 Scottish Premiership</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-toy14_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toy14-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hull_City">Hull City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hull City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andy_Robertson_(19596759769).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg/170px-Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="215" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg/255px-Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg/340px-Andy_Robertson_%2819596759769%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1714" data-file-height="2168" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andy_Robertson_(19596759769).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Robertson playing for <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in 2015</div></div></div>
<p>In July 2014, Dundee United accepted an offer of £2.85&#160;million for Robertson from English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The transfer was completed on 29 July, as Robertson signed a three-year contract with Hull.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> As Stan Ternent, Hull City's chief scout explained, "I'd been looking at <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Armstrong" title="Stuart Armstrong">Stuart Armstrong</a> but [Robertson] was the no brainer … he'd a history with Celtic and he was always a determined lad given how he'd recovered from his setbacks. You could see straight away he had ability and he can only get better."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made his competitive debut on the opening day of the season in a 1–0 victory away to <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>, in which he made a goal-line clearance.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Robertson quickly settled at Hull and established himself in the side, winning the club's Player of the Month award for August 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He made 24 appearances in his debut season at the <a href="/wiki/KC_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="KC Stadium">KC Stadium</a>, but was unable to prevent the team from being relegated to <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">the Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TFT_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TFT-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite the release of several other first-team players, Robertson opted to stay at City. His first goal for the club came on 3 November 2015 away to <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a> where he opened the scoring in a 2–0 win to put Hull top of the Championship table on goal difference.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He started the <a href="/wiki/2016_Football_League_Championship_play-off_Final" title="2016 Football League Championship play-off Final">2016 Championship play-off Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>, which Hull won 1–0 to secure promotion to the Premier League.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The team spent just one season back in the top flight, however, before being relegated yet again.<sup id="cite_ref-TFT_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TFT-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robertson and fellow Hull defender <a href="/wiki/Harry_Maguire" title="Harry Maguire">Harry Maguire</a> were recommended by <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> scout <a href="/wiki/Steve_Walsh_(scout)" title="Steve Walsh (scout)">Steve Walsh</a> to transfer in a combined £20 million deal, but Everton rejected the proposal.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Liver-RM_(10).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg/170px-Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="221" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg/255px-Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg/340px-Liver-RM_%2810%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="715" data-file-height="929" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Liver-RM_(10).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Robertson playing for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2018 UEFA Champions League Final">2018 UEFA Champions League Final</a></div></div></div>
<p>On 21 July 2017, Robertson signed a long-term deal with <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> for an initial fee of £8&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On 19 August, he made his debut in a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> winning the Man of the Match award.<sup id="cite_ref-places_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Robertson started the 2017–18 season as understudy to <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Moreno" title="Alberto Moreno">Alberto Moreno</a>, but got a run of games when the Spaniard was injured in December. His performance in the 4–3 victory against league leaders <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> on 14 January earned praise from the Liverpool supporters.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club on the final match day of the 2017–18 season against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a> in a 4–0 victory.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robertson continued to play regularly for Liverpool during the 2018–19 season when Liverpool were runners-up in the Premier League.<sup id="cite_ref-contract_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contract-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He signed a new contract with the club in January 2019, which is due to run until 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-contract_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contract-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He has been hailed as the best left back in the Premier League by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Kilbane" title="Kevin Kilbane">Kevin Kilbane</a><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and the best left back in the world by <a href="/wiki/Phil_Neville" title="Phil Neville">Phil Neville</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Neville_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neville-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On 25 April, he was named in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Team of the Year">PFA Team of the Year</a> alongside Liverpool teammates, <a href="/wiki/Trent_Alexander-Arnold" title="Trent Alexander-Arnold">Trent Alexander-Arnold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sadio_Man%C3%A9" title="Sadio Mané">Sadio Mané</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgil_van_Dijk" title="Virgil van Dijk">Virgil van Dijk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 June 2019, Robertson played the full 90 minutes of the <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2019 UEFA Champions League Final">Champions League final</a> as Liverpool beat <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> to secure their sixth European Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-2019_CL_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019_CL-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He became the first Scot to win the tournament since <a href="/wiki/Darren_Fletcher" title="Darren Fletcher">Darren Fletcher</a> (an unused substitute in <a href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2008 UEFA Champions League Final">2008</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and the first to actually be on the field in a winning team since <a href="/wiki/Paul_Lambert" title="Paul Lambert">Paul Lambert</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="1997 UEFA Champions League Final">1997</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2019_CL_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019_CL-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the European champions' first home game of the Champions League for the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2019–20 UEFA Champions League">2019–20</a> campaign on 2 October, Robertson scored his first European goal in a dramatic 4–3 victory against <a href="/wiki/FC_Red_Bull_Salzburg" title="FC Red Bull Salzburg">Red Bull Salzburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On 2 November 2019, Robertson scored his second Premier League goal for Liverpool, equalising in the 87th minute against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, with Liverpool eventually winning 2–1 in stoppage time.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the course of the 2019–20 domestic campaign, Robertson contributed 2 goals and 12 assists for Liverpool.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg/170px-Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="211" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg/255px-Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg/340px-Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg 2x" data-file-width="761" data-file-height="944" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andrew_Robertson_2019.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Robertson playing for <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> in 2019</div></div></div>
<p>Robertson was first selected by the <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Scotland national under-21 football team">Scotland under-21 team</a> in October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut as a substitute in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Slovakia national under-21 football team">Slovakia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> 
Robertson was selected for the under-21 team in November 2015, as the full national team did not have a fixture.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robertson was first named in the full <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> squad for a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> on 5 March 2014. He came on as a substitute in the second half, as Scotland won 1–0 in Warsaw.<sup id="cite_ref-poland_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poland-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Scotland manager <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Strachan" title="Gordon Strachan">Gordon Strachan</a> said afterwards "Andy came on there and the first time he picked it up he drove about 30 yards. I thought 'that's fantastic'. Absolutely no grey area, I'm going to do what I do. I loved seeing that first touch."<sup id="cite_ref-poland_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poland-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Robertson made his first start for the national team in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> on 28 May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robertson scored his first international goal in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> at <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Park" title="Celtic Park">Celtic Park</a> in November 2014, scoring Scotland's only goal as they were beaten 3–1.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 September 2018, Robertson was appointed as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scotland_national_football_team_captains" title="List of Scotland national football team captains">Scotland captain</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Alex_McLeish" title="Alex McLeish">Alex McLeish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> After a 2–1 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_football_team" title="Israel national football team">Israel</a> in October 2018, Robertson said that both he and fellow left-back <a href="/wiki/Kieran_Tierney" title="Kieran Tierney">Kieran Tierney</a> were being played out of position in the 3–5–2 system adopted by Alex McLeish to accommodate both players.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020" title="UEFA Euro 2020">UEFA Euro 2020</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020_qualifying_Group_I" title="UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group I">qualifier</a> against Cyprus on 8 June 2019, Robertson scored the opening goal of the game with a long-range strike. Scotland eventually won 2–1.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In November, he was one of five Scotland players to withdraw from the national squad due to injury.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, with Robertson again as Captain, Scotland qualified for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020" title="UEFA Euro 2020">UEFA Euro 2020</a> following a 5-4 win on penalties against Serbia. It will be Scotland's first major tournament appearance since 1998, ending a 22 year streak of not qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robertson is considered by former England defender Phil Neville to be one of the best left-backs in the world, due to his pace, energy, and <a href="/wiki/Cross_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross (football)">crossing</a> ability.<sup id="cite_ref-Neville_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neville-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A1bio_Aur%C3%A9lio" title="Fábio Aurélio">Fábio Aurélio</a>, a predecessor for Liverpool in the same position, singled out Robertson for praise, saying "One thing that calls my attention is that he's always improving, he's not comfortable in his situation being first choice," and that "The two full-backs of Liverpool right now are unbelievable. We can contest in any kind of way."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Former Liverpool captain <a href="/wiki/Sir_Kenny_Dalglish" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Kenny Dalglish">Sir Kenny Dalglish</a> has said "For me, there are few, if any, better than him in his position."<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Robertson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robertson is engaged to Rachel Roberts,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> who gave birth to their son, Rocco, on 26 August 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> In January 2019, the couple welcomed their second child and first daughter, Aria.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, Robertson donated a signed Liverpool shirt of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Firmino" title="Roberto Firmino">Roberto Firmino</a> to a young boy who had given his pocket money to a local <a href="/wiki/Food_bank" title="Food bank">food bank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">The Sunday Times</a></i> reported in March 2020 that Robertson had donated significant sums to food banks in the Glasgow area.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2020, he and fellow Liverpool full-back <a href="/wiki/Trent_Alexander-Arnold" title="Trent Alexander-Arnold">Trent Alexander-Arnold</a> starred in their own digital series on IGTV called 'Wingmen'.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in 2020, Robertson released his first book, mainly focusing on Liverpool's historic <a href="/wiki/2019-20_Liverpool_F.C._season" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 Liverpool F.C. season"> 2019-20 Campaign</a>. All proceeds from the book will go to Robertson's foundation, which helps underprivileged children in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
