<p><b>Matthijs de Ligt</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[mtis d lxt]</a></span>; born 12 August 1999) is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-soccerway_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soccerway-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 September 2016, De Ligt made his debut for <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>'s senior team in a cup game against <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a>. He scored from a corner after 25 minutes, making him the second-youngest goalscorer ever behind <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Seedorf" title="Clarence Seedorf">Clarence Seedorf</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 24 May, he became the youngest player ever (17 years and 285 days old) to play in a major European final when he started against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2017 UEFA Europa League Final">2017 UEFA Europa League Final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 17 December 2018, De Ligt won the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Boy_(award)" title="Golden Boy (award)">Golden Boy</a> award, becoming the first defender to win the award.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The following season, he helped Ajax capture a domestic double and reach the semi-finals of the Champions League; his performances earned him a move to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> side Juventus, and saw him win the <a href="/wiki/Kopa_Trophy" title="Kopa Trophy">Kopa Trophy</a> in 2019. He won the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> title  during his first season with the Turin side.
</p><p>In 2017, De Ligt made his debut for the Netherlands at the age of 17, making him the youngest player to start for the national team since 1931.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg/170px-Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg/255px-Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg/340px-Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg 2x" data-file-width="994" data-file-height="1286" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Matthijs_de_Ligt.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>De Ligt played for Ajax between 2008 and 2019.</div></div></div>
<p>De Ligt joined the Ajax youth academy when he was 9 years old from his local club in <a href="/wiki/Abcoude" title="Abcoude">Abcoude</a>, just outside of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>. At first the coaches at the youth academy thought he was too slow and out of shape, but he was given a chance to develop in the academy and went on to prove his quality.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317:_Jong_Ajax_and_development"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17:_Jong_Ajax_and_development">2016–17: Jong Ajax and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016–17: Jong Ajax and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>He made his debut for Jong Ajax on 8 August 2016 in an <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a> game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Emmen" title="FC Emmen">FC Emmen</a>, playing the full game.<sup id="cite_ref-soccerway2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soccerway2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> During the season, De Ligt played in 17 matches for Jong Ajax.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 September, De Ligt made his debut for Ajax's senior team in a cup game against <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a>. He scored from a corner after 25 minutes, making him the second-youngest goalscorer ever behind <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Seedorf" title="Clarence Seedorf">Clarence Seedorf</a>; Ajax won the game 5–0.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 24 October, Ajax announced through <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that De Ligt had been promoted to the senior squad.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De Ligt quickly established himself in the first team after being promoted and went on to play in 11 league matches and nine in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-matthijs-de-ligt_career_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matthijs-de-ligt_career-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 24 May, he became the youngest player ever (17 years and 285 days old) to play in a major European final when he started against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2017 UEFA Europa League Final">2017 UEFA Europa League Final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9319:_Breakthrough_and_captaincy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–19:_Breakthrough_and_captaincy">2017–19: Breakthrough and captaincy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2017–19: Breakthrough and captaincy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the sale of <a href="/wiki/Davinson_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Davinson Sánchez">Davinson Sánchez</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in August 2017, De Ligt became a first team starter. He was named the youngest <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> of Ajax in March 2018 after injury to club captain <a href="/wiki/Joel_Veltman" class="mw-redirect" title="Joel Veltman">Joel Veltman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De Ligt appeared in 37 matches in all competitions and scored three goals. He started in all of his 31 league matches and played 90 minutes in all but one of them (he was substituted in the 33rd minute against <a href="/wiki/Vitesse_Arnhem" class="mw-redirect" title="Vitesse Arnhem">Vitesse Arnhem</a> due to an injury).<sup id="cite_ref-matthijs-de-ligt_career_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matthijs-de-ligt_career-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Due to De Ligt's performances for Ajax, he was linked with many of Europe's top clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 December 2018, De Ligt won the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Boy_(award)" title="Golden Boy (award)">Golden Boy</a> award, becoming the first defender to win the award.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On 13 February 2019, De Ligt became the youngest ever captain in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> knockout game at 19 years and 186 days old, in a game against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 27 February 2019, he played his 100th official game for Ajax in a 0–3 <a href="/wiki/Klassieker" class="mw-redirect" title="Klassieker">Klassieker</a> win against <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> in the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">Dutch national cup</a>, becoming the youngest Ajax player ever to reach this milestone.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 16 April 2019, De Ligt scored the winning goal in the Champions League quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> to send Ajax on to the semi-finals of the competition for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1996–97 UEFA Champions League">1996–97</a> edition of the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His goal also saw him become the youngest Dutch player to score in the knockout stages since <a href="/wiki/Nordin_Wooter" title="Nordin Wooter">Nordin Wooter</a> in 1996 and the second-youngest ever defender to score after <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%ABl_Matip" title="Joël Matip">Joël Matip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Deligt-juventus-2019_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg/190px-Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="190" height="223" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg/285px-Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg/380px-Deligt-juventus-2019_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="568" data-file-height="667" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Deligt-juventus-2019_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>De Ligt with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> blocking a shot against <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">Lokomotiv Moscow</a> during the 2019–20 season</div></div></div>
<p>On 18 July 2019, De Ligt signed for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> on a five-year contract for a fee of €75&#160;million, to be paid in five annual installments, with additional costs of €10.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He made his official debut for the club in a 4–3 home win over <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> in Serie A on 31 August.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On 3 November, he scored his first for the club in a 1–0 away win over cross-city rivals <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Derby_della_Mole" title="Derby della Mole">Derby della Mole</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 25 March 2017, De Ligt made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Dutch national team</a> in a 2–0 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_A" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group A">2018 FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match. National team coach, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Blind" title="Danny Blind">Danny Blind</a>, called De Ligt up for the national team despite having only started two league games for Ajax. At the age of 17, he became the youngest player to start for the national team since 1931.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Nations_League_A" title="2018–19 UEFA Nations League A">UEFA Nations League</a>, De Ligt started and played 90 minutes in all four group matches against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> as the Netherlands won the group and qualified for <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Nations_League_Finals" title="2019 UEFA Nations League Finals">the inaugural Nations League Finals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In the semi-finals on 5 June, he scored a header against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> as his team secured a spot in <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Nations_League_Final" title="2019 UEFA Nations League Final">the final</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> where the Netherlands would be defeated 0–1 by <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De Ligt is considered by pundits to be among the most highly regarded young prospects in world football, as demonstrated by his victory in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Golden_Boy_(award)" title="Golden Boy (award)">Golden Boy</a> award, which is assigned to the most impressive young player in European football.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having played as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> in his youth,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> De Ligt is both a physical and technically gifted right-footed centre-back,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pianetamilan1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pianetamilan1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> who is known for his clean tackling, anticipation, marking, height, strength, speed and accurate passing, which enables him to start attacking plays from the back; moreover, his height allows him to be a major threat from set pieces, whilst his strength means that he is unlikely to be pushed off the ball, particularly in aerial duels.<sup id="cite_ref-pianetamilan1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pianetamilan1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> His playing style is often compared to that of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Piqu%C3%A9" title="Gerard Piqué">Gerard Piqué</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> he has also been likened to <a href="/wiki/Jan_Vertonghen" title="Jan Vertonghen">Jan Vertonghen</a>, due to his "similar forward surges, a propensity to create an extra man in midfield, and calmness on the ball," and to compatriot <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Koeman" title="Ronald Koeman">Ronald Koeman</a>, courtesy of his "powerful shot and body strength."<sup id="cite_ref-How_brilliant_is_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-How_brilliant_is-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A 2019 <i><a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>.com</i> profile described De Ligt with the following words: "A natural leader, De Ligt is an elegant two-footed central defender. Dominant in the air, he is blessed with strength, fine distribution and composure, and is able to play out from the back thanks to his confidence in possession, while his intelligent positioning helps him deny pacy forwards. With his ability to read the game, he could even play in midfield. His leadership also stands him out: he was 18 when he was made Ajax skipper."<sup id="cite_ref-How_brilliant_is_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-How_brilliant_is-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> That same year, Paul Wilkes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> also noted that "De Ligt’s ability to play out from the back and organise his fellow teammates are valuable commodities in a time when the lack of quality defenders has been highlighted."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Former <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Carragher" title="Jamie Carragher">Jamie Carragher</a> praised De Ligt for his "physical attributes" and "leadership qualities". Furthermore, Carragher went on to say he plays "like he's 24 or 25" indicating maturity and strength.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Former Netherlands centre-back <a href="/wiki/Jaap_Stam" title="Jaap Stam">Jaap Stam</a> said De Ligt: "He's got composure on the ball, he's aggressive tactically, he sees the game, he reads the game well and he's got that driving force for himself as well in what you need to achieve and where you want to go to."<sup id="cite_ref-How_brilliant_is_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-How_brilliant_is-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Upon signing with Juventus, one of the club's former managers, <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Lippi" title="Marcello Lippi">Marcello Lippi</a>, instead said of the defender's precocious performances: "I've seen plenty of greats, like <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nesta" title="Alessandro Nesta">Alessandro Nesta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franco_Baresi" title="Franco Baresi">Franco Baresi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Maldini" title="Paolo Maldini">Paolo Maldini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Ferrara" title="Ciro Ferrara">Ciro Ferrara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Cannavaro" title="Fabio Cannavaro">Fabio Cannavaro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Materazzi" title="Marco Materazzi">Marco Materazzi</a>. I've never seen anyone like that at his age."<sup id="cite_ref-How_brilliant_is_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-How_brilliant_is-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Giacomo Chiuchiolo described De Ligt as a "complete" and talented young defender, noting that although he is not exceptionally quick, he possesses excellent positioning and an ability to read the game, among his other technical and physical qualities.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Despite his talent, however, he has come under criticism at times in the media for being inconsistent, due to his tendency to commit occasional lapses or errors, although his Juventus defensive teammate <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Bonucci" title="Leonardo Bonucci">Leonardo Bonucci</a> has stated that he believes that De Ligt will overcome these weaknesses with time, as he matures and gains more experience with age, and successfully adapts to the team's new defensive strategy under manager <a href="/wiki/Maurizio_Sarri" title="Maurizio Sarri">Maurizio Sarri</a>, who uses a <a href="/wiki/Zonal_marking" class="mw-redirect" title="Zonal marking">zonal system</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Man_to_man_marking" class="mw-redirect" title="Man to man marking">man–to–man marking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthijs_de_Ligt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De Ligt is in a relationship with Dutch model Annekee Molenaar.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 January 2021, de Ligt tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">COVID-19</a> amid its <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Italy" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Italy">pandemic in Italy</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> he recovered by 23 January.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
