<p><b>Carlos Henrique Casimiro</b> (born 23 February 1992), known as <b>Casemiro</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (<span style="font-size:85%;">Brazilian Portuguese:&#160;</span><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[kazimiu]</a></span>), is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> for Spanish club <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil national team</a>.
</p><p>Formed at <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo</a>, where he scored 11 goals in 112 official games, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> in 2013, and also spent a season on loan at <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a>. He was part of the Real Madrid squad that won four <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> titles in five seasons, from <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Champions League">2013–14</a> to <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">2017–18</a>. In total he has won 14 major trophies at Real Madrid, including four <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, two <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> and three <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="FIFA Club World Cup">FIFA Club World Cup</a> trophies. 
</p><p>A full international since 2011, Casemiro was in <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>'s squad at three <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a> tournaments as well as the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>, winning the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2019 Copa América">2019 Copa América</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SW_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SW-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casemiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="S.C3.A3o_Paulo"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="São_Paulo">São Paulo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casemiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: São Paulo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9_dos_Campos" title="São José dos Campos">São José dos Campos</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a>, Casemiro was a product of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a>'s youth system. From the age of 11 upwards, he acted as <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> to its sides;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> he was known as "Carlão" – an augmentative form of his first name in Portuguese – early on, and would be called up for the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Casemiro made his <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Série A</a> debut on 25 July 2010, in an away loss against <a href="/wiki/Santos_FC" title="Santos FC">Santos FC</a>. He scored his first goal as a professional on 15 August, helping to a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Cruzeiro_Esporte_Clube" title="Cruzeiro Esporte Clube">Cruzeiro Esporte Clube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 7 April 2012, Casemiro scored the first goal of a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Mogi_Mirim_Esporte_Clube" title="Mogi Mirim Esporte Clube">Mogi Mirim Esporte Clube</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Arena_Barueri" title="Arena Barueri">Arena Barueri</a> in <a href="/wiki/2012_Campeonato_Paulista" title="2012 Campeonato Paulista">that year</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Paulista" title="Campeonato Paulista">Campeonato Paulista</a> after <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">replacing</a> the injured <a href="/wiki/Fabr%C3%ADcio_de_Souza" title="Fabrício de Souza">Fabrício</a> early on, but was later <a href="/wiki/Ejection_(sports)" title="Ejection (sports)">sent off</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> São Paulo also <a href="/wiki/2012_Copa_Sudamericana" title="2012 Copa Sudamericana">won</a> the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Sudamericana" title="Copa Sudamericana">Copa Sudamericana</a>, with the player making one substitute appearance in a 5–0 home success against <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Club Universidad de Chile</a> in the quarter-final second leg on 7 November.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_Madrid">Real Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casemiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Real Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Shahter-Reak_M_2015_(16)_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Shahter-Reak_M_2015_%2816%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="435" data-file-height="429" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Shahter-Reak_M_2015_(16)_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Casemiro playing for <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> in 2015</div></div></div>
<p>In late January 2013, Casemiro was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> in Spain, being assigned to <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_Castilla" title="Real Madrid Castilla">the B-team</a> in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He played his first game in the competition on 16 February, starting in a 1–3 defeat at <a href="/wiki/CE_Sabadell_FC" title="CE Sabadell FC">CE Sabadell FC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Casemiro made his <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> debut on 20 April 2013, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 2 June he scored his first goal in Europe, opening the reserves' 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AD_Alcorc%C3%B3n" title="AD Alcorcón">AD Alcorcón</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_di_St%C3%A9fano_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfredo di Stéfano Stadium">Alfredo di Stéfano Stadium</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> eight days later, the move was made permanent for four years and a fee of <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_real" title="Brazilian real">R$</a>18.738&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Casemiro was loaned to <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> on 19 July 2014, in a <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Primeira_Liga" title="2014–15 Primeira Liga">season</a>-long loan.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He totalled 40 games overall for the Portuguese, netting four times<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> including a <a href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct free kick">free kick</a> on 10 March 2015 in a 4–0 home win (<a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">5–1 aggregate</a>) over <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">FC Basel</a> in the last 16 of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 June 2015, Casemiro returned to Real Madrid who activated his buyback clause,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and two months later his contract was extended until 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On 13 March of the following year, he scored his first competitive goal for the <i>Merengues</i>, heading home an 89th-minute <a href="/wiki/Corner_kick" title="Corner kick">corner kick</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%A9" title="Jesé">Jesé</a> in a 2–1 victory at <a href="/wiki/UD_Las_Palmas" title="UD Las Palmas">UD Las Palmas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After being mostly a reserve player under <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Rafael Benítez">Rafael Benítez</a>, Casemiro became first-choice under his successor <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and contributed with 11 appearances in <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">the season's Champions League</a> as the tournament ended in win. In <a href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2016 UEFA Champions League Final">the final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, he featured the full 120 minutes in a 1–1 draw in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a> triumph).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Casemiro scored four goals in 25 matches in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_La_Liga" title="2016–17 La Liga">2016–17 campaign</a>, helping his team win the national championship for the first time in five years.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He scored a long-range strike in the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2017 UEFA Champions League Final">Champions League final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus FC</a>, helping his side to a 4–1 victory;<sup id="cite_ref-UEFA_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UEFA-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> he again found the net on 8 August 2017, putting his team ahead in an eventual <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Super_Cup" title="2017 UEFA Super Cup">2–1 defeat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Super_Cup" title="UEFA Super Cup">UEFA Super Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GUARDIAN_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GUARDIAN-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">2017–18 Champions League</a>, Casemiro made 13 appearances while scoring one goal,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> when Madrid won their third consecutive and 13th overall title in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was a regular starter during the league season, as Real Madrid won the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_La_Liga" title="2019–20 La Liga">2019–20 La Liga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Liga_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Liga-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casemiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Casemiro.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Casemiro.jpg/170px-Casemiro.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="268" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Casemiro.jpg/255px-Casemiro.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Casemiro.jpg/340px-Casemiro.jpg 2x" data-file-width="484" data-file-height="764" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Casemiro.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Casemiro playing for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_National_Football_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazil National Football Team">Brazil</a></div></div></div>
<p>After appearing with the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-20_football_team" title="Brazil national under-20 football team">under-20s</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2011_South_American_U-20_Championship" title="2011 South American U-20 Championship">2011 South American Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 World Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Casemiro made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil senior side</a> on 14 September 2011, in a 0–0 draw against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, aged just 19.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He was named by manager <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a> in the Brazilian squad for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a>, but did not play any matches in the quarter-final exit in Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 May 2016, he was named among the 23-man list for the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Centenario" title="Copa América Centenario">Copa América Centenario</a> to be held in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018, Casemiro was selected by manager <a href="/wiki/Tite_(football_manager)" title="Tite (football manager)">Tite</a> for <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">the upcoming edition</a> of the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut in <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">the competition</a> on 17 June, playing 60 minutes in a 1–1 group stage draw to <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2019, he was included in Brazil's 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2019 Copa América">2019 Copa América</a> on home soil.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In the final group game at the <a href="/wiki/Arena_Corinthians" title="Arena Corinthians">Arena Corinthians</a> against <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a>, he scored his first international goal to open a 5–0 victory, but was also sent off for two yellow cards and suspended from the next match.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He started in Brazil's 3–1 victory over Peru in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Final" title="2019 Copa América Final">2019 Copa América Final</a> on 7 July, at the <a href="/wiki/Maracan%C3%A3_Stadium" title="Maracanã Stadium">Maracanã Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casemiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%; ;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1012376600"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"For the balance of the team, he is without a doubt [Real Madrid's] most important player ... He has changed the face of Madrid."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—<a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Diego_Simeone" title="Diego Simeone">Diego Simeone</a> before the <a href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2016 UEFA Champions League Final">2016 UEFA Champions League Final</a><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Casemiro mainly plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> although he has also been deployed in a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Box-to-box_midfielder" title="Midfielder">box-to-box role</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">centre</a> of the pitch,<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-responsible_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsible-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> or even as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a> on occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> While he is not particularly talented or skilful from a technical standpoint<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> (although he was able to improve in this area under manager <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> during their time at Real Madrid together),<sup id="cite_ref-rock_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Casemiro is an intelligent, physically strong, mobile, aggressive, and hard-<a href="/wiki/Tackle_(football_move)#Association_football" title="Tackle (football move)">tackling</a> midfielder, with an extremely high work-rate.<sup id="cite_ref-rock_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-responsible_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsible-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> While he is mainly known for his ability to provide balance to his teams by supporting his more offensive-minded teammates defensively, and distributing the ball to them accurately after winning back possession, he is also a well-rounded midfielder, who possesses a powerful shot from outside the penalty area, and reliable distribution, as well as an ability to get forward with his runs off the ball, or start attacks with his passing; these characteristics also enable him to contribute offensively, or even score goals, in addition to breaking up plays.<sup id="cite_ref-responsible_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsible-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-well-rounded_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-well-rounded-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tuttomercatoweb_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tuttomercatoweb-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Moreover, his quick reactions, as well as his excellent positional sense, ability to read the game, and good anticipation, enable him to excel in a holding midfield role in front of the defence, by cutting out counter-attacks and shielding the back-line.<sup id="cite_ref-responsible_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsible-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rock_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Casemiro_effect_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Casemiro_effect-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Often described as a "destroyer" in the media,<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> he has drawn praise from pundits for his energetic and combative style of play,<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> as well as his tactical awareness and abilities as a ball winner, which enable him to cover ground, track back, and press opponents.<sup id="cite_ref-well-rounded_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-well-rounded-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ranking_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ranking-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Casemiro_effect_61-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Casemiro_effect-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> His tenacity on the pitch, exemplified by his fierce challenges and stamina,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> have led <i><a href="/wiki/Marca_(newspaper)" title="Marca (newspaper)">Marca</a></i> to nickname him "The Tank."<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his footballing abilities, he also stands out for his competitive spirit, mentality, consistency, and determination on the pitch.<sup id="cite_ref-rock_52-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Despite mainting a good disciplinary record, he has, however, also come into criticism in the media at times over his excessively aggressive challenges on the pitch.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> Casemiro's playing style has drawn comparisons with that of former Real Madrid defensive midfielder <a href="/wiki/Claude_Mak%C3%A9l%C3%A9l%C3%A9" title="Claude Makélélé">Claude Makélélé</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rock_52-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> as well as compatriot <a href="/wiki/Toninho_Cerezo" title="Toninho Cerezo">Toninho Cerezo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tuttomercatoweb_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tuttomercatoweb-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
