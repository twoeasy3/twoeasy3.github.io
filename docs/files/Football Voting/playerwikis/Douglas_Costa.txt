<p><b>Douglas Costa de Souza</b> (<span style="font-size:85%;">Brazilian Portuguese:&#160;</span><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[dowls kst]</a></span>; born 14 September 1990) is a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> for <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, on loan from <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil national team</a>. He is known for his <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribbling</a> skills, speed, acceleration, and <a href="/wiki/Cross_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross (football)">crossing</a> ability.
</p><p>Costa started his career with <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a>, before moving to <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a> in January 2010 for a fee of €6&#160;million. He won numerous trophies with Shakhtar, including a domestic treble in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk_season" title="2010–11 FC Shakhtar Donetsk season">2010–11 season</a> (<a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2010–11 Ukrainian Premier League">Premier League</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ukrainian_Cup" title="2010–11 Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Super_Cup" title="Ukrainian Super Cup">Super Cup</a>). In 2015, he joined Bayern for €30&#160;million, where he won two consecutive league titles. He was loaned out to Juventus in 2017, and won a domestic double with the club in his first season. On 7 June 2018, Juventus made his loan move permanent; he added another two consecutive league titles in his next two seasons with the club, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> title. On deadline day of the 2020 summer transfer window, Costa returned to Bayern Munich on loan.
</p><p>A full international since 2014, Costa represented Brazil at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Gr.C3.AAmio"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Grêmio">Grêmio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Grêmio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sapucaia_do_Sul" title="Sapucaia do Sul">Sapucaia do Sul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>, Douglas began his career with <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a>, joining the club at the age of 11.<sup id="cite_ref-ebio_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebio-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut for the club at the age of 18 in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Botafogo_de_Futebol_e_Regatas" title="Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas">Botafogo</a> on 4 October 2008, scoring his first goal for the club in the same match.<sup id="cite_ref-ebio_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebio-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He completed the 2008 season with one goal in six matches.<sup id="cite_ref-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 14 June 2009 he was given a red card in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 29 November 2009, he netted the first goal in a 4–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Barueri_Futebol" title="Grêmio Barueri Futebol">Grêmio Recreativo Barueri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He joined Ukrainian side <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</a> on 10 January 2010. He made 37 appearances for Grêmio, including 28 league appearances, scoring twice.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shakhtar_Donetsk">Shakhtar Donetsk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shakhtar Donetsk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="2009.E2.80.9310_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–10_season">2009–10 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–10 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 10 January 2010, Costa signed a five-year contract with Shakhtar Donetsk in a deal worth €6&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His debut came in a <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> tie against English side <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> on 18 February which Shakhtar lost 2–1. He came on as a substitute in the 75th minute, replacing <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A1dson" title="Jádson">Jádson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Donbass_Arena" title="Donbass Arena">home</a> debut came in the return leg on 25 February; he came on as 53rd-minute replacement for <a href="/wiki/Willian_(footballer,_born_1988)" title="Willian (footballer, born 1988)">Willian</a>, assisting Jádson for Shakhtar's equaliser in a 1–1 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Shakhtar lost the tie 3–2 on aggregate. He scored his first goal for the club in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalist_Kharkiv" title="FC Metalist Kharkiv">Metalist Kharkiv</a> on 14 March 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On 28 March, he scored a goal in injury time in a 3–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/FC_Arsenal_Kyiv" title="FC Arsenal Kyiv">Arsenal Kyiv</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> then scored a goal in a 3–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Chornomorets_Odesa" title="FC Chornomorets Odesa">Chornomorets Odesa</a> on 25 April.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 1 May, Costa scored a late goal in Shakhtar's 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalurh_Zaporizhya" title="FC Metalurh Zaporizhya">Metalurh Zaporizhya</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He scored Shakhtar's third goal in a 3–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/SC_Tavriya_Simferopol" title="SC Tavriya Simferopol">Tavriya Simferopol</a> on 9 May.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his first <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk_season" title="2009–10 FC Shakhtar Donetsk season">season</a> with Shakhtar, Douglas made 15 appearances, including 13 league appearances, scoring five goals, all of which came in the league. Shakhtar won the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2009–10 Ukrainian Premier League">2009–10 Premier League</a> title, Costa's first trophy with the club.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2010.E2.80.9311_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–11_season">2010–11 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–11 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg/170px-Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="252" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg/255px-Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/92/Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg 2x" data-file-width="298" data-file-height="442" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_de_Souza.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Costa in training with Shakhtar Donetsk in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>Costa's first appearance in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk_season" title="2010–11 FC Shakhtar Donetsk season">2010–11 season</a> came in the 7–1 <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Super_Cup" title="Ukrainian Super Cup">Super Cup</a> win against Tavriya on 4 July.<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Super_Cup_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Super_Cup-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On 30 July, he netted a brace in a 3–1 victory against Arsenal Kyiv.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He scored the fourth goal in Shakhtar's 5–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/PFC_Sevastopol" class="mw-redirect" title="PFC Sevastopol">PFC Sevastopol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On 28 September, he scored a penalty, and assisted two <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Adriano" title="Luiz Adriano">Luiz Adriano</a> goals in a 3–0 <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> victory over Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">Braga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On 28 September, he scored a penalty in a 4–0 win against Arsenal Kyiv.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 16 February, he netted the second goal, and assisted the third for Luiz Adriano, in a 3–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Shakhtar won the tie 6–2 on aggregate. On 1 April, he scored a late goal in a 3–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Illichivets_Mariupol" class="mw-redirect" title="FC Illichivets Mariupol">Illichivets Mariupol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He came on as a 95th-minute substitute in Shakhtar's 2–0 <a href="/wiki/2011_Ukrainian_Cup_Final" title="2011 Ukrainian Cup Final">Ukrainian Cup Final</a> victory over Dynamo Kyiv on 25 May.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shakhtar managed to end the season as treble winners, winning the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2010–11 Ukrainian Premier League">Premier League</a>, <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Ukrainian_Cup" title="2010–11 Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a> and the Super Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Costa made a total of 39 appearances for the club, scoring seven goals.<sup id="cite_ref-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2010_Super_Cup_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Super_Cup-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Twenty-seven of the appearances and five of the goals came in the league.<sup id="cite_ref-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2011.E2.80.9312_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–12_season">2011–12 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011–12 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_costa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Douglas_costa.jpg/220px-Douglas_costa.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Douglas_costa.jpg/330px-Douglas_costa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Douglas_costa.jpg/440px-Douglas_costa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="552" data-file-height="700" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_costa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Costa playing for Shakhtar in 2011</div></div></div>
<p>Shakhtar started the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk_season" title="2011–12 FC Shakhtar Donetsk season">2011–12 season</a> with a 3–1 loss in the Super Cup to <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a> on 5 July. Costa played the full 90 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-2011_Super_Cup_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011_Super_Cup-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> His first goal of the season came on 9 September, scoring the final goal in a 5–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/FC_Volyn_Lutsk" title="FC Volyn Lutsk">Volyn Lutsk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On 2 October, he scored the equalising goal in a 4–1 victory over Zorya Luhansk.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 6 November, he scored the first goal in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Obolon_Kyiv" title="FC Obolon Kyiv">Obolon Kyiv</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On 27 November, he scored a penalty to open the scoring in a 5–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Karpaty_Lviv" title="FC Karpaty Lviv">Karpaty Lviv</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On 2 December, he netted a goal against Arsenal Kyiv in a comprehensive 5–0 win.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On 11 December, he scored the first goal in a 4–0 win against Kryvbas.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 27 April, he scored a 90th-minute goal in a 4–3 victory over Volyn in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ukrainian_Cup" title="2011–12 Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He made a 62nd-minute substitute appearance, replacing <a href="/wiki/Henrikh_Mkhitaryan" title="Henrikh Mkhitaryan">Henrikh Mkhitaryan</a>, in Shakhtar's 2–1 extra time victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalurh_Donetsk" title="FC Metalurh Donetsk">Metalurh Donetsk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the season, Shakhtar won the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Ukrainian Premier League">Premier League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ukrainian_Cup" title="2011–12 Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a>, Costa's third Premier League title success in his third year with the club.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He played 34 matches, scoring six goals.<sup id="cite_ref-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2011_Super_Cup_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011_Super_Cup-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Twenty-seven of the appearances and all six of the goals came in the league.<sup id="cite_ref-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douglas_Costa_»_Club_matches-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2012.E2.80.9313_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–13_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa2013.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Douglas_Costa2013.jpg/220px-Douglas_Costa2013.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Douglas_Costa2013.jpg/330px-Douglas_Costa2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Douglas_Costa2013.jpg/440px-Douglas_Costa2013.jpg 2x" data-file-width="570" data-file-height="515" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa2013.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Costa playing for Shakhtar in 2013</div></div></div>
<p>Costa opened the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk_season" title="2012–13 FC Shakhtar Donetsk season">2012–13 season</a> with a goal in Shakhtar's 2–0 win against Metalurh Donetsk in the Super Cup, his second success in the Super Cup with Shakhtar.<sup id="cite_ref-2012_Super_Cup_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2012_Super_Cup-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first league goal of the season on 3 November 2012 when he converted a 20th-minute penalty against <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalurh_Zaporizhya" title="FC Metalurh Zaporizhya">Metalurh Zaporizhya</a>; the match finished 2–0 thanks to a late goal by Luiz Adriano.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 24 November, Douglas came off the bench in the 55th minute, replacing team captain <a href="/wiki/Darijo_Srna" title="Darijo Srna">Darijo Srna</a>, and netted a goal seven minutes later when he was played in by Armenian forward Henrikh Mkhitaryan, as Shakhtar defeated <a href="/wiki/FC_Hoverla_Uzhhorod" title="FC Hoverla Uzhhorod">Hoverla Uzhhorod</a> 5–1.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the group stages of the season's edition of <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>, Costa only managed three substitute appearances, totaling 23 minutes played, as Shakhtar finished second in the group behind <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">Serie A</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, while dumping <a href="/wiki/2012_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2012 UEFA Champions League Final">holders</a> <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> out of the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Following an underwhelming first half of the 2012–13 campaign, Costa came off the bench to score a vital goal in Shakhtar's 2–2 first leg draw with <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> in the Champions League Round of 16 tie on 13 February 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2013.E2.80.9314_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–14_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Costa made his season debut in the Super Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-2013_Super_Cup_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2013_Super_Cup-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In mid-season, he helped Shakhtar win the <a href="/wiki/2014_United_Supercup" title="2014 United Supercup">2014 United Supercup</a>, a tournament between the top-two placed clubs from Russia and Ukraine, becoming the joint top scorer of the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He helped the club win the Premier League and was a joint assist leader of the league with nine assists.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2014.E2.80.9315_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–15_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Costa played 33 games in all competitions and scored 5 goals in his final season at Shakhtar Donetsk, including a goal in a 7–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">BATE Borisov</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> He also played in both matches in the Champions League round of 16 against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span id="2015.E2.80.9316_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–16_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 1 July 2015, <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> announced they had signed Costa<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> for a transfer fee of €30&#160;million. Costa signed a five-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-FC_Bayern_holt_Costa_für_30_Millionen_Euro_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FC_Bayern_holt_Costa_für_30_Millionen_Euro-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The €30&#160;million transfer fee was the fourth-highest in Bayern's history.<sup id="cite_ref-FC_Bayern_holt_Costa_für_30_Millionen_Euro_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FC_Bayern_holt_Costa_für_30_Millionen_Euro-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He was given the number 11 shirt previously worn by <a href="/wiki/Xherdan_Shaqiri" title="Xherdan Shaqiri">Xherdan Shaqiri</a>. He made his debut as a starter on 1 August in the <a href="/wiki/2015_DFL-Supercup" title="2015 DFL-Supercup">2015 DFL-Supercup</a> away to <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a>, crossing for <a href="/wiki/Arjen_Robben" title="Arjen Robben">Arjen Robben</a> to open the scoring in an eventual 1–1 draw; he netted his attempt in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shoot-out</a>, but his team lost nonetheless.<sup id="cite_ref-Nicklas_Bendtner_emerges_as_Wolfsburg&#39;s_unlikely_hero_in_DFL-Supercup_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nicklas_Bendtner_emerges_as_Wolfsburg&#39;s_unlikely_hero_in_DFL-Supercup-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Costa scored his first Bundesliga goal in his first Bundesliga game in a 5–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On 21 May 2016, Costa scored the winning kick of the penalty shootout against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/2016_DFB-Pokal_Final" title="2016 DFB-Pokal Final">2016 DFB-Pokal Final</a> and secure a league and <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_DFB-Pokal" title="2015–16 DFB-Pokal">cup</a> double for Bayern.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Costa enjoyed a good first season at Bayern scoring seven goals in 43 matches throughout all competitions.<sup id="cite_ref-Nicklas_Bendtner_emerges_as_Wolfsburg&#39;s_unlikely_hero_in_DFL-Supercup_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nicklas_Bendtner_emerges_as_Wolfsburg&#39;s_unlikely_hero_in_DFL-Supercup-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2015–16_season_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2015–16_season-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> He started the season in great form and had 12 assists in his first 13 games. Then he picked up a muscle injury, and missed the last four games before the winter break. After the winter break, Costa could not regain his former form.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In 27 Bundesliga matches, Costa scored four goals and had 14 assists.
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Costa got a new manager when Bayern hired <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Ancelotti" title="Carlo Ancelotti">Carlo Ancelotti</a>. He had enjoyed regular playing time under <a href="/wiki/Pep_Guardiola" title="Pep Guardiola">Pep Guardiola</a> and was a regular starter, but Ancelotti preferred <a href="/wiki/Franck_Rib%C3%A9ry" title="Franck Ribéry">Franck Ribéry</a> as the left winger. Costa had a hamstring and knee injuries during the season and played in 34 matches in all competitions. He scored seven goals in those matches and matching his scoring tally from last season and did play nearly 1,400 fewer minutes than the season before.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Costa had a fall-out with the Bayern board when he complained publicly about his lack of playing time under Ancelotti. Costa said "I speak to the club often," he said. "We talk about my situation, if I'll stay here or if I have to find another team to play for. I'm not always that happy here. But soon we will find a situation." Shortly before, Bayern president <a href="/wiki/Uli_Hoene%C3%9F" title="Uli Hoeneß">Uli Hoeneß</a>, had accused Costa of attempting to earn himself an improved contract when Costa publicly revealed he was unsure about where his future lay and cited interest from the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9318_season:_Loan_to_Juventus"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–18_season:_Loan_to_Juventus">2017–18 season: Loan to Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2017–18 season: Loan to Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 12 July 2017, Costa joined <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> on <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> until the end of the 2017–18 season, for €6&#160;million. Juventus has an option to make the move permanent on 1 July 2018, for an additional €40 million fee (plus €1M in bonuses).<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Costa_signs_for_Juve_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Costa_signs_for_Juve-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> On 13 August, he made his club debut, coming on as a substitute in a 3–2 defeat to <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_Supercoppa_Italiana" title="2017 Supercoppa Italiana">2017 Supercoppa Italiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> On 14 October, he scored his first goal for the club in a 2–1 home defeat to Lazio.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> In his debut season he scored four goals in Serie A in 31 appearances, and two goals in five appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Coppa_Italia" title="2017–18 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> (one of which came in Juventus's 4–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2018 Coppa Italia Final">2018 Coppa Italia Final</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a> in Rome, on 9 May), recording six goals in 47 appearances across all competitions, as Juventus won a domestic double; he also led the league in dribbles (106) and recorded the second-most assists (14) in <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Serie_A" title="2017–18 Serie A">Serie A</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Permanent_Contract_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Permanent_Contract-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Juve_make_Costa_deal_permanent_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Juve_make_Costa_deal_permanent-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="2018.E2.80.9319_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–19_season">2018–19 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2018–19 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_(06.11.2019).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg/180px-Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="323" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg/270px-Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg/360px-Douglas_Costa_%2806.11.2019%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="624" data-file-height="1120" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_(06.11.2019).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Douglas Costa in 2019 in <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2019–20 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a></div></div></div>
<p>On 7 June 2018, Juventus made his loan move permanent, signing Costa to a four-year deal for a fee of €40 million plus bonuses.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> On 16 September 2018, in a 2–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a> in Serie A, Costa was sent-off in injury time following an altercation with <a href="/wiki/Federico_Di_Francesco" title="Federico Di Francesco">Federico Di Francesco</a>. The two players had clashed in the build-up to Sassuolo's 91st minute consolation goal, scored by <a href="/wiki/Khouma_Babacar" title="Khouma Babacar">Khouma Babacar</a>, following a late challenge on Di Francesco by Costa. As both players got up, Costa appeared to elbow Di Francesco and later attempted to headbutt him, before finally spitting in his face. Costa was initially booked for the headbutt, but the controversial incident was later reviewed by <a href="/wiki/Video_Assistant_Referee" class="mw-redirect" title="Video Assistant Referee">VAR</a> officials, who alerted the referee of his subsequent actions, and as a result, he was sent off. Juventus manager <a href="/wiki/Massimiliano_Allegri" title="Massimiliano Allegri">Massimiliano Allegri</a> commented in the post-match press conference that Costa will be fined by the club. Costa later wrote a public apology on <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a>, stating "I want to apologise to all the Juventus fans for this over the top reaction I had during today’s game. I also apologise to my teammates, who are always by my side, in good and bad times. It was ugly, I am aware of that and I apologise to everyone for that. I want to make clear that this isolated conduct has nothing to do with what I’ve shown throughout my career."<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Two days later on 18 September, Costa was given a four-match Serie A ban for his elbow, attempted headbutt and spit on Federico Di Francesco.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2019.E2.80.9320_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–20_season">2019–20 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2019–20 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 6 November 2019, Costa scored his first Champions League goal with Juventus in a 2–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">Lokomotiv Moscow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> On 20 July 2020, he made his 100th appearance for Juventus in a 2–1 home win over Lazio.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2020.E2.80.9321_season:_Loan_return_to_Bayern"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–21_season:_Loan_return_to_Bayern">2020–21 season: Loan return to Bayern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2020–21 season: Loan return to Bayern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 5 October 2020, on deadline day, Bayern Munich announced the return of Costa on a <a href="/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_FC_Bayern_Munich_season" title="2020–21 FC Bayern Munich season">season</a>-long loan.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> On 28 November, he scored his first goal of the season in a 3–1 away win over <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg/170px-Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="262" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg/255px-Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg/340px-Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="582" data-file-height="898" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Douglas_Costa_Brazil.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Costa on international duty, facing <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> in 2018</div></div></div>
<p>In November 2014, Costa was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil national team</a> for the first time by coach <a href="/wiki/Dunga" title="Dunga">Dunga</a>, making his international debut against <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_football_team" title="Turkey national football team">Turkey</a> in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2015, Costa was included in Brazil's <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_squads#Brazil" title="2015 Copa América squads">23-man squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> In the team's opening match, he came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Diego_Tardelli" title="Diego Tardelli">Diego Tardelli</a> and scored a stoppage-time winning goal against <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a>, his first for the <i>Seleção</i>. In the quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> in <a href="/wiki/Concepci%C3%B3n,_Chile" title="Concepción, Chile">Concepción</a>, he replaced former Shakhtar teammate Willian for the final 30 minutes of a 1–1 draw, and then missed in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_records_and_statistics#List_of_all_penalty_shoot-outs_that_have_occurred_in_the_Finals_tournament" title="Copa América records and statistics">penalty shoot-out</a> which eliminated Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> On 17 November, Costa scored his second goal for Brazil in 3–0 victory against Peru for <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2018 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Costa was initially included in <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Centenario_squads#Brazil" title="Copa América Centenario squads">Brazil's squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Centenario" title="Copa América Centenario">Copa América Centenario</a>, but was forced to withdraw from the squad in late May 2016 after sustaining an injury to his left thigh; he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kak%C3%A1" title="Kaká">Kaká</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 June 2016, then-technical director <a href="/wiki/Gilmar_Rinaldi" title="Gilmar Rinaldi">Gilmar Rinaldi</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Football_Confederation" title="Brazilian Football Confederation">Brazilian Football Confederation</a>, "shortly after he was sacked", stated that Bayern would let Costa play at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bayern_Munich_to_release_Douglas_Costa_to_play_in_Olympics,_Brazil_say_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bayern_Munich_to_release_Douglas_Costa_to_play_in_Olympics,_Brazil_say-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018, he was named in <a href="/wiki/Tite_(football_manager)" title="Tite (football manager)">Tite</a>'s final <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_squads#Brazil" title="2018 FIFA World Cup squads">23-man squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a> in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Costa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="272" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2170" data-file-height="2685" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:20180610_FIFA_Friendly_Match_Austria_vs._Brazil_850_0197_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Douglas Costa about to cross the ball in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria</a> in 2018</div></div></div>
<p>Regarded as a promising prospect in his youth, in 2010, Costa was included in <a href="/wiki/Don_Bal%C3%B3n" title="Don Balón">Don Balón</a>'s list of the 100 best young players born after 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> A diminutive player, with a slender build and an eye for goal, he initially played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> in his early career, but later established himself as a versatile winger, capable of playing on either flank, and of adapting himself to several different formations, due to his energy and work-rate, as well as his powerful and accurate striking ability from distance with either foot; however, he usually favours the right wing, as it enables to cut into the centre onto his stronger left foot.<sup id="cite_ref-Costa_signs_for_Juve_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Costa_signs_for_Juve-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Costa-Bayern_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Costa-Bayern-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> Nicknamed the <i><a href="/wiki/Flash_(Barry_Allen)" title="Flash (Barry Allen)">Flash</a></i>, he is mainly known for his speed, agility, and explosive acceleration, both with and without the ball, as well as his <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribbling</a> skills, technical ability, creativity, and trickery in possession, which enable him to beat opponents in one on one situations.<sup id="cite_ref-Costa_signs_for_Juve_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Costa_signs_for_Juve-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Costa-Bayern_81-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Costa-Bayern-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup><sup
