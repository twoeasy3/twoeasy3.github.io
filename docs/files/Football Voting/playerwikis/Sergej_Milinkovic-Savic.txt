<p><b>Sergej Milinkovi-Savi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Serbian Cyrillic alphabet">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr-Cyrl"> -</span>, <small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA/Serbo-Croatian">[serêj milkoit sit, srej-]</a></span>; born 27 February 1995), also known <a href="/wiki/Mononymous_person" title="Mononymous person">mononymously</a> as <b>Sergej</b>, is a Serbian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for Italian club <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">Serbia national team</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Milinkovi-Savi was born into a sporting family in <a href="/wiki/Lleida" title="Lleida">Lleida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> where his father <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Milinkovi%C4%87" title="Nikola Milinkovi">Nikola Milinkovi</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> played football professionally at the time. His mother, Milana Savi, was a professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player. Sergej also made his first football steps with <a href="/wiki/Sporting_CP" title="Sporting CP">Sporting CP</a>, while his father was playing in Portugal.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Milinkovi-Savi is the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Vanja_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87" title="Vanja Milinkovi-Savi">Vanja Milinkovi-Savi</a>, a fellow footballer who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>. In additional to his Serbian citizenship, Milinkovi is also a <a href="/wiki/Naturalized_citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Naturalized citizen">naturalized citizen</a> of Spain.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vojvodina">Vojvodina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vojvodina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Milinkovi-Savi came through the youth academy of <a href="/wiki/FK_Vojvodina" title="FK Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a>. He was one of the midfielders who emerged from the youth squad that won two consecutive national youth championship titles, alongside <a href="/wiki/Mijat_Ga%C4%87inovi%C4%87" title="Mijat Gainovi">Mijat Gainovi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neboj%C5%A1a_Kosovi%C4%87" title="Nebojša Kosovi">Nebojša Kosovi</a>. Consequently, Milinkovi-Savi signed his first professional contract with the club on 26 December 2012, penning a three-year deal.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Milinkovi-Savi made his senior debut in a 3–0 away loss to <a href="/wiki/FK_Jagodina" title="FK Jagodina">Jagodina</a> on 23 November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal in a 1–1 away draw with <a href="/wiki/FK_Spartak_Subotica" title="FK Spartak Subotica">Spartak Subotica</a> on 9 March 2014. In total, Milinkovi-Savi made 13 league appearances and scored three goals in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serbian_SuperLiga" title="2013–14 Serbian SuperLiga">2013–14 season</a>. He also helped Vojvodina win the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serbian_Cup" title="2013–14 Serbian Cup">2013–14 Serbian Cup</a> in the club's centennial year.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genk">Genk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Genk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, Milinkovi-Savi signed a five-year contract with Belgian club <a href="/wiki/K.R.C._Genk" title="K.R.C. Genk">Genk</a> and was given the number 20 shirt.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He made his competitive debut for the club in a 1–1 home league draw with <a href="/wiki/Cercle_Brugge_K.S.V." title="Cercle Brugge K.S.V.">Cercle Brugge</a> on 2 August 2014. Milinkovi-Savi scored his first goal for Genk in a 1–1 away league draw against <a href="/wiki/K.S.C._Lokeren_Oost-Vlaanderen" title="K.S.C. Lokeren Oost-Vlaanderen">Lokeren</a> on 18 January 2015. He scored a total of five league goals in 24 appearances during the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Belgian_Pro_League" title="2014–15 Belgian Pro League">2014–15 season</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lazio">Lazio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lazio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dnepr-Lazio_(2)_-_%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%BF%D0%B8%D1%8F.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Dnepr-Lazio_%282%29_-_%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%BF%D0%B8%D1%8F.jpg/150px-Dnepr-Lazio_%282%29_-_%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%BF%D0%B8%D1%8F.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="398" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/67/Dnepr-Lazio_%282%29_-_%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%BF%D0%B8%D1%8F.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="157" data-file-height="417" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dnepr-Lazio_(2)_-_%D0%BA%D0%BE%D0%BF%D0%B8%D1%8F.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Milinkovi-Savi lining up for <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> in 2015</div></div></div>
<p>On 31 July 2015, it was announced that Milinkovi-Savi would join Italian club <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut for the side in a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> in the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">Champions League play-off round</a> on 18 August 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Milinkovi-Savi scored his first goal for Lazio in a 1–1 <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Europa_League_group_stage" title="2015–16 UEFA Europa League group stage">Europa League</a> draw with <a href="/wiki/FC_Dnipro_Dnipropetrovsk" class="mw-redirect" title="FC Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk</a> on 17 September 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> goal in a 3–1 away win over <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> on 9 January 2016. Throughout his <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_S.S._Lazio_season" title="2015–16 S.S. Lazio season">debut season</a>, Milinkovi-Savi made 35 appearances and scored three goals for Lazio in all competitions.
</p><p>In his <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_S.S._Lazio_season" title="2016–17 S.S. Lazio season">second season</a> at Lazio, Milinkovi-Savi scored seven goals from 39 appearances (league and cup).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Milinkovi-Savi represented <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-19_football_team" title="Serbia national under-19 football team">Serbia</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2013 UEFA Under-19 Championship</a>, winning the gold medal. He played the full 90 minutes in four out of five games, including the final match of the competition against <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">France</a> that Serbia won 1–0.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Subsequently, Milinkovi-Savi appeared at the <a href="/wiki/2014_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2014 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2014 UEFA Under-19 Championship</a>, as the team was eliminated in the semi-final by <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-19_football_team" title="Portugal national under-19 football team">Portugal</a> after penalties. He missed Serbia's last spot-kick in the penalty shoot-out.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The following summer, Milinkovi-Savi was one of <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-20_football_team" title="Serbia national under-20 football team">Serbia</a>'s most influential players at the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>, as the team won the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He appeared in six out of seven games and scored one goal against <a href="/wiki/Mali_national_under-20_football_team" title="Mali national under-20 football team">Mali</a> in the group stage. Due to his performances during the tournament, Milinkovi-Savi was awarded the Bronze Ball.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2017, Lazio prevented Milinkovi-Savi from playing at the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2015, Milinkovi-Savi received his first call-up to the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">full squad</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Radovan_%C4%86ur%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Radovan uri">Radovan uri</a> for Serbia's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He remained an unused substitute in both games. In May 2016, Milinkovi-Savi was selected by newly appointed manager <a href="/wiki/Slavoljub_Muslin" title="Slavoljub Muslin">Slavoljub Muslin</a> for friendly matches against <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_national_football_team" title="Cyprus national football team">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_football_team" title="Israel national football team">Israel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, after failing to receive any playing time in the first two games, Milinkovi-Savi left the team in agreement with Muslin who said that some other players better fit into his tactical formations.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 30 October 2017, it was announced that Serbia caretaker manager <a href="/wiki/Mladen_Krstaji%C4%87" title="Mladen Krstaji">Mladen Krstaji</a> included Milinkovi-Savi in the squad for friendlies against <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2018, he was selected in the Serbian squad for the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 World Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> playing all three group stage matches.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Milinkovi-Savi scored his first two senior international goals on 8 October 2020, settling a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a> in the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020_qualifying_play-offs#Path_C" title="UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying play-offs">Euro 2020 qualifying play-off</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergej_Milinkovi%C4%87-Savi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sergej Milinkovi-Savi has established himself as a "complete <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a>", in the modern sense of the term, which earned him the moniker the <i><a href="/wiki/Paul_Pogba" title="Paul Pogba">Paul Pogba</a> of Serbia</i>. A versatile player, he is capable of playing in several midfield roles, including as a "trequartista", or in a <a href="/wiki/Holding_role" class="mw-redirect" title="Holding role">holding role</a>, although he is usually deployed as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a>, in the "<a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Mezzala" title="Midfielder">mezzala</a>" role. His style of play has been compared by pundits to that of players such as <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yaya_Tour%C3%A9" title="Yaya Touré">Yaya Touré</a>, as he blends the traditional attacking movement, positional sense, and finishing ability of an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>, with the height and physicality of a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Centre-forward" title="Forward (association football)">target man</a> or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>; as such, he also is effective in the air, and is capable of scoring goals with his head as well as either foot, by making late runs from behind into the penalty area, or striking from distance. He possesses good technique, tactical intelligence, vision, and passing ability, despite his lack of pace, as well as tenacity and an excellent defensive work-rate, which enables him to help out at both ends of the pitch, carry the ball, and start attacking plays after winning back possession.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
