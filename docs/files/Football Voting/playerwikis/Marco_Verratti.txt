<p><b>Marco Verratti</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[marko verratti]</a></span>; born 5 November 1992) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a> for French <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> club <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint_Germain_F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Paris Saint Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national team</a>.
</p><p>A creative, hard-working, and technically gifted <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a>, Verratti began his career with Italian club <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> in 2008, where he soon rose to prominence as one of the best young <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielders</a> in Europe, helping the team to win the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_B" title="2011–12 Serie B">2011–12 Serie B</a> title, and winning the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Bravo_Award" title="Bravo Award">Bravo Award</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-skyit_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skyit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His playing style drew comparisons with <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a>, due to his passing ability, vision, and control, as well as his similar transition from  the role of <i><a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">trequartista</a></i> to that of a <i><a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Deep-lying_playmaker" title="Midfielder">regista</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In July 2012, he transferred to French side <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>, where he won seven <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> titles from 2013 to 2020, among other domestic and individual trophies.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At international level, Verratti represented the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy under-21 football team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>, winning a runners-up medal, and being named to the all-star squad for the tournament. At senior level, he made his <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> debut in 2012, and represented his country at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Verratti was born in <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila" title="L&#39;Aquila">L'Aquila</a>. As a child, he supported <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> and idolised <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Del_Piero" title="Alessandro Del Piero">Alessandro Del Piero</a>, the team's <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian international</a> offensive <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Verratti's talent was noticed at an early age, and he was made offers to join the youth academies of <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> and <a href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" class="mw-redirect" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a>, but joined his local club <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> for €5,000. After an impressive performance for Pescara's under-16 team against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> club offered €300,000 to sign the midfielder, but Verratti decided to remain at Pescara.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pescara">Pescara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pescara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marco_Verratti.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Marco_Verratti.jpg/170px-Marco_Verratti.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Marco_Verratti.jpg/255px-Marco_Verratti.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Marco_Verratti.jpg/340px-Marco_Verratti.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1988" data-file-height="2567" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marco_Verratti.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Verratti playing for <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> in 2012</div></div></div>
<p>The first youth team Verratti was affiliated with was that of <a href="/wiki/Manoppello" title="Manoppello">Manoppello</a>, a historic team of Pescara, that in 2001 merged with the newly born Manoppello Arabona. He remained there until 2006 when he entered the youth of <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a>.
</p><p>Verratti made his first team debut in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Serie_B" title="2008–09 Serie B">2008–09 season</a> at the age of 15 years and 9 months. In the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serie_B" title="2009–10 Serie B">2009–10 season</a>, he appeared more regularly for Pescara, and since then became a key player in the first team. His performances generated national media coverage as a potential star of the future and Italian international.<sup id="cite_ref-skyit_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skyit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With <a href="/wiki/Zden%C4%9Bk_Zeman" title="Zdenk Zeman">Zdenk Zeman</a> as manager, Verratti excelled as a <a href="/wiki/Playmaker#Deep-lying_playmakers" title="Playmaker">deep-lying playmaker</a> in a Pescara team which won the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_B" title="2011–12 Serie B">2011–12 Serie B</a> title playing "the best football in Italy", earning promotion to <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serie_A" title="2012–13 Serie A">Serie A</a> the following season.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After helping Pescara to promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>, Verratti received the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Bravo_Award" title="Bravo Award">Bravo Award</a> for the best player under the age of 21 in Europe, and was reportedly wanted by <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> and Juventus, as well as <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Ancelotti" title="Carlo Ancelotti">Carlo Ancelotti</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Oscar_del_Calcio" class="mw-redirect" title="Oscar del Calcio">2012 AIC Gran Gala del Calcio</a>, he was elected the best player of the 2011–12 Serie B season, along with former Pescara teammates <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Immobile" title="Ciro Immobile">Ciro Immobile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Insigne" title="Lorenzo Insigne">Lorenzo Insigne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-corriere.it_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere.it-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paris_Saint-Germain">Paris Saint-Germain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Paris Saint-Germain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="2012.E2.80.9313:_Debut_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–13:_Debut_season">2012–13: Debut season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–13: Debut season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PSG_Reims_2079.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/PSG_Reims_2079.JPG/170px-PSG_Reims_2079.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="189" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/PSG_Reims_2079.JPG/255px-PSG_Reims_2079.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/PSG_Reims_2079.JPG/340px-PSG_Reims_2079.JPG 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="889" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PSG_Reims_2079.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Verratti with <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> in March 2013.</div></div></div>
<p>On 18 July 2012, Verratti signed a five-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On 2 September 2012, Verratti made his competitive debut for PSG in an away Ligue 1 match against <a href="/wiki/Lille_O.S.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Lille O.S.C.">Lille</a>. Twelve days later, he  <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assist (football)">assisted</a> <a href="/wiki/Javier_Pastore" title="Javier Pastore">Javier Pastore</a>'s opening goal in his first competitive appearance at the <a href="/wiki/Parc_des_Princes" title="Parc des Princes">Parc des Princes</a>, a 2–0 Ligue 1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kiev-PSG_(9).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg/170px-Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="265" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg/255px-Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg/340px-Kiev-PSG_%289%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="374" data-file-height="583" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kiev-PSG_(9).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Verratti playing for Paris Saint-Germain against <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a> in 2012</div></div></div>
<p>On 18 September 2012, he made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> debut in a <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League group stage">4–1 Group A defeat</a> of <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He ended his first season in the French capital by winning the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Ligue_1" title="2012–13 Ligue 1">2012–13 Ligue 1</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_French_football_champions" title="List of French football champions">title</a>. On 20 August 2013, Verratti signed a one-year extension to his contract with PSG, keeping him at the club until 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2013.E2.80.9314:_Individual_awards"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–14:_Individual_awards">2013–14: Individual awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–14: Individual awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Verratti's second season with PSG was even more successful than the first as the team retained its Ligue 1 title and won the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Coupe_de_la_Ligue" title="2013–14 Coupe de la Ligue">Coupe de la Ligue</a> and the
<a href="/wiki/2013_Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="2013 Trophée des Champions">2013 Trophée des Champions</a>. Verratti was named as the Ligue 1 Young Player of the Year and included in the Ligue 1 Team of the Year for <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Ligue_1" title="2013–14 Ligue 1">2013–14</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2014.E2.80.932017:_First_goals_and_contract_extension"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2017:_First_goals_and_contract_extension">2014–2017: First goals and contract extension</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014–2017: First goals and contract extension">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 30 September 2014, Verratti scored his first competitive goal for PSG, a header in a 3&#8211;2 home win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On 18 January 2015, Verratti scored his first goal in Ligue 1, in a 4–2 home win over <a href="/wiki/Evian_Thonon_Gaillard_F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Evian Thonon Gaillard F.C.">Evian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2016, Verratti signed a 3-year extension to his contract, keeping him at the club until 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.932019:_European_and_domestic_performances"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–2019:_European_and_domestic_performances">2017–2019: European and domestic performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017–2019: European and domestic performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 6 March 2018, Verratti was sent off after acquiring a second yellow card in the second leg of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> Round of 16 fixture against the eventual champions <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Real Madrid went on to win the game 2–1 and eliminated PSG with an aggregate score of 5–2.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 April 2019, Verratti scored the opening goal from a <a href="/wiki/Kylian_Mbapp%C3%A9" title="Kylian Mbappé">Kylian Mbappé</a> assist in a 3–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Nantes" title="FC Nantes">Nantes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Coupe_de_France" title="2018–19 Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> semi-finals, which allowed PSG to advance to the <a href="/wiki/2019_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="2019 Coupe de France Final">final of the competition</a>; during the same match, he also provided an assist for his team's final goal, which was scored by <a href="/wiki/Dani_Alves" title="Dani Alves">Dani Alves</a>. This goal was Verratti's first of the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C._season" title="2018–19 Paris Saint-Germain F.C. season">2018–19 season for PSG</a>, and also his first goal ever in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2019.E2.80.9320:_Domestic_treble_and_European_final"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–20:_Domestic_treble_and_European_final">2019–20: Domestic treble and European final</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2019–20: Domestic treble and European final">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following PSG's 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais_F.C." title="Stade Rennais F.C.">Rennes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="2019 Trophée des Champions">2019 Trophée des Champions</a> on 3 August,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Verratti became the player with the most titles with the club, with 22 trophies in total (six Ligue 1 titles, four Coupe de France titles, five Coupe de la Ligue titles, and seven Trophée des Champions titles).<sup id="cite_ref-Storico_Verratti_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Storico_Verratti-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Verratti_signs_new_deal_at_PSG_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Verratti_signs_new_deal_at_PSG-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In October, he signed a three–year contract extension with the club, which would tie him to PSG until June 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-Verratti_signs_new_deal_at_PSG_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Verratti_signs_new_deal_at_PSG-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 12 January 2020, Verratti made his 300th appearance for PSG in a 3–3 home draw against Monaco in Ligue 1.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On 18 February, he became the club's all-time appearance holder in the Champions League, when he made his 58th appearance in the competition in a 2–1 away loss in the first leg of their round of 16 draw against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, overtaking the previous record holder <a href="/wiki/Paul_Le_Guen" title="Paul Le Guen">Paul Le Guen</a> at 57.<sup id="cite_ref-Storico_Verratti_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Storico_Verratti-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In April, PSG were assigned the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Ligue_1" title="2019–20 Ligue 1">2019–20 Ligue 1</a> title after the season was ended prematurely due to the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>; at the time of the League's suspension, PSG were in first place, with a twelve–point lead over second–placed Marseille. This was Verratti's record seventh Ligue 1 title with the club, which saw him equal the all–time individual record of most Ligue 1 title victories, along with his club teammate <a href="/wiki/Thiago_Silva" title="Thiago Silva">Thiago Silva</a>, which was jointly held by <a href="/wiki/Herv%C3%A9_Revelli" title="Hervé Revelli">Hervé Revelli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Michel_Larqu%C3%A9" title="Jean-Michel Larqué">Jean-Michel Larqué</a> of <a href="/wiki/AS_Saint-%C3%89tienne" title="AS Saint-Étienne">Saint-Étienne</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%A9gory_Coupet" title="Grégory Coupet">Grégory Coupet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juninho_Pernambucano" title="Juninho Pernambucano">Juninho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Govou" title="Sidney Govou">Sidney Govou</a> of <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-seventh_heaven_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seventh_heaven-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> PSG went on to win a domestic treble,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> but lost out in the <a href="/wiki/2020_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2020 UEFA Champions League Final">Champions League final</a> 1–0 to <a href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> on 23 August; Verratti made a substitute appearance during the match.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2020.E2.80.9321:_Injury_and_new_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–21:_Injury_and_new_season">2020–21: Injury and new season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2020–21: Injury and new season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In October 2020, Verratti suffered a <a href="/wiki/Quadriceps_femoris_muscle" title="Quadriceps femoris muscle">quadriceps</a> injury while playing for <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He made his return to play as a substitute in a 1–0 UEFA Champions League win against <a href="/wiki/RB_Leipzig" title="RB Leipzig">RB Leipzig</a> on 24 November 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Cap_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cap (association football)">uncapped</a> Verratti was included into <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a>'s preliminary 32-man <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, being one of only two Serie B players (the other being <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino's</a> <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Ogbonna" title="Angelo Ogbonna">Angelo Ogbonna</a>) to be part of it.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was subsequently cut from the squad on 28 May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 15 August 2012, Verratti made his debut with the Italian senior team in a 2–1 friendly loss against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently made his first competitive appearance in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>, appearing as a second-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Giaccherini" title="Emanuele Giaccherini">Emanuele Giaccherini</a> in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> on 7 September.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 February 2013, Verratti scored his first international goal in a friendly against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in Amsterdam, a 91st-minute equalizer that made the final score 1–1.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He took part with the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy U-21</a> side at the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>, where the <i>Azzurrini</i> finished as runner-up to <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spain</a>. His performances saw him included in <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>'s all-star squad for the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 June 2014, Verratti was selected in Italy's 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In Italy's opening match of the tournament, he made his first competitive start for the <i>Azzurri</i> on the left side of a midfield diamond with Juventus players <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Marchisio" title="Claudio Marchisio">Claudio Marchisio</a> and Roma vice-captain <a href="/wiki/Daniele_De_Rossi" title="Daniele De Rossi">Daniele De Rossi</a> in his team's opening game of the tournament, a 2–1 victory over England in <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>. During the match, he was involved in Marchisio's opening goal; after receiving <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Candreva" title="Antonio Candreva">Antonio Candreva</a>'s short corner, he passed the ball out wide towards Pirlo, who drew his marker with him. Pirlo let the ball pass in between his legs to Marchisio, leaving him with space to score with a low drive from outside the area.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> However, Verratti did not appear in Italy's second group match, a 1–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> he started in Italy's final group match against <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a>, which also ended in a 1–0 loss,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and as a result, Italy were eliminated in the first round of the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 May 2016, it was confirmed Verratti would miss <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a> after a long lasting injury from a sports hernia which would require surgery on 16 May, sidelining him for two months.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Upon recovering from his injury, Verratti returned to the team under Italy's new manager <a href="/wiki/Gian_Piero_Ventura" title="Gian Piero Ventura">Gian Piero Ventura</a> for an international friendly match against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> in Bari on 1 September, making a substitute appearance in the <i>Azzurri</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 3–1 loss.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 26 March 2019, Verratti scored his second international goal in a 6–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national football team">Liechtenstein</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying</a> match.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Verratti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%; ;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1012376600">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"He's one of the best midfielders in the world, and plays a little bit in the same way as I tried to do with Barcelona and the national team. He likes having the ball. He also has perfect mastery over long and short passing, he can play the final ball, and he doesn't lose the ball that easily. He's really a very high-class player."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—<a href="/wiki/Xavi" title="Xavi">Xavi</a> on Verratti in 2015<sup id="cite_ref-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>A quick, creative, agile, and technically gifted <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a>, with notable <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribbling</a> skills, quick reactions, and close ball control, Verratti is known for his confidence on the ball and ability to retain possession in tight spaces when under pressure, due to his low centre of gravity and resulting balance.<sup id="cite_ref-skyit_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skyit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Monaco-PSG_D-1:_Verratti_indispensable_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monaco-PSG_D-1:_Verratti_indispensable-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Meet_Marco_Verratti_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meet_Marco_Verratti-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> His excellent vision and range of passing enable him to create chances for teammates with accurate long passes, or control the tempo of his team's play in midfield through precise, short exchanges.<sup id="cite_ref-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Verratti_is_one_of_world&#39;s_best-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Monaco-PSG_D-1:_Verratti_indispensable_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monaco-PSG_D-1:_Verratti_indispensable-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ilcentro.gelocal.it_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilcentro.gelocal.it-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Although naturally right-footed,<sup id="cite_ref-tuttocalciatori_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tuttocalciatori-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> he is capable of playing with either foot.<sup id="cite_ref-vivoperlei_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vivoperlei-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Due to his attributes and playing style, Verratti has been compared to Italy legend <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a>, in particular after making the same transition from <i><a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">trequartista</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Deep-lying_playmaker" title="Midfielder">regista</a></i> that the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> winner had also made earlier on in his career,<sup id="cite_ref-fourfourtwo_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourfourtwo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paragone_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paragone-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fifa_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fifa-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> although he has also been deployed in a new, more advanced role alongside Pirlo on occasion, as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#False_attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">false-attacking midfielder</a>, in particular under Cesare Prandelli's tenure with the Italy national team.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Initially regarded as one of the most talented young players of his generation,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Verratti quickly developed into one of the best playmaking <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielders</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his playmaking skills, he is also a hard-working, dynamic, and well-rounded midfielder, who is known for his aggression, defensive skills, and ball-winning abilities, despite his small physique and diminutive stature, which also enables him to be deployed in a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">holding role</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ilcentro.gelocal.it_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilcentro.gelocal.it-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tuttocalciatori_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tuttocalciatori-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vivoperlei_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vivoperlei-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> He drawn criticism over his disciplinary record, as well as his tendency to commit an excessive number of fouls, argue with officials, and pick up unnecessary bookings.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
