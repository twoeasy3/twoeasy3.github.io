<p><b>Lorenzo Insigne</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[lorntso insie]</a></span>; born 4 June 1991) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a>, for which he is <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national team</a>.
</p><p>Insigne began his professional career with Napoli in 2009, making his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut in 2010, but was later sent on consecutive season loan spells to <a href="/wiki/Cavese_1919" title="Cavese 1919">Cavese</a>, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Foggia" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Foggia">Foggia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a>, before returning to Napoli in 2012. Insigne is capable of playing on either flank, or through the centre, but is usually deployed as left <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Winger" title="Forward (association football)">winger</a>. He is known in particular for his creativity, speed and technical ability, as well as his accuracy from free-kicks.<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Insigne has represented the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy national under-21 team</a>, with which he won a runner-up medal at the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>. He made his debut for the senior team in September 2012, and has represented Italy at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.
</p>

<p>In 2006, at age 15, Insigne was signed by <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> from Olimpia Sant'Arpino for 1,500.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, he made his debut for the <i>Primavera</i> team, with whom he scored 15 goals in the 200910 season.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was first called-up to the first team under <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Donadoni" title="Roberto Donadoni">Roberto Donadoni</a>, playing some friendlies in the summer of 2009, before making his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut under <a href="/wiki/Walter_Mazzarri" title="Walter Mazzarri">Walter Mazzarri</a>, on 24 January 2010, in a 20 win away to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He played the remainder of the season on loan at <a href="/wiki/S.S._Cavese_1919" class="mw-redirect" title="S.S. Cavese 1919">Cavese</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a>, where he made 10 appearances.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loans_to_Foggia_and_Pescara">Loans to Foggia and Pescara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loans to Foggia and Pescara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The following season, Insigne was loaned to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Foggia" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Foggia">Foggia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a>. He scored his first professional goal on 14 August, in the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia_Lega_Pro" class="mw-redirect" title="Coppa Italia Lega Pro">Coppa Italia Lega Pro</a> match against <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila_Calcio_1927" title="L&#39;Aquila Calcio 1927">L'Aquila</a>, while on 29 August, he scored his first league goal in a 23 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Lucchese_Libertas_1905" class="mw-redirect" title="A.S. Lucchese Libertas 1905">Lucchese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Under the manager <a href="/wiki/Zden%C4%9Bk_Zeman" title="Zdenk Zeman">Zdenk Zeman</a>, he totalled 19 goals in the league, in addition to seven goals scored in Coppa Italia Lega Pro.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 July 2011, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a> in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, now managed by Zeman.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut in the Italian second division on 26 August 2011 in the opening round against <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona" class="mw-redirect" title="Hellas Verona">Hellas Verona</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and on 4 September scored his first goal for Pescara away against <a href="/wiki/Modena_F.C._2018" title="Modena F.C. 2018">Modena</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Insigne finished the season with 18 goals, making him the second top-scorer of the season behind teammate <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Immobile" title="Ciro Immobile">Ciro Immobile</a>, and 14 assists.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Among the key players of Pescara's title-winning season and promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>, Insigne was subsequently awarded the "Best Player" of the Serie B season, along with teammates Immobile and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Verratti" title="Marco Verratti">Marco Verratti</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-areanapoli.it_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-areanapoli.it-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Napoli">Napoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Napoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of the season, Insigne returned to Napoli, choosing the number 24 jersey.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 16 September 2012, he scored his first goal in Serie A, after entering as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Edinson_Cavani" title="Edinson Cavani">Edinson Cavani</a> in a 31 home win over <a href="/wiki/Parma_Calcio_1913" title="Parma Calcio 1913">Parma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Four days later, he made his debut in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_club_competitions" class="mw-redirect" title="UEFA club competitions">UEFA club competitions</a>, starting in a 40 win against Swedish side <a href="/wiki/AIK_Fotboll" title="AIK Fotboll">AIK Fotboll</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UEFA_Profile_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UEFA_Profile-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Insigne struggled to get a run of consecutive games at many points in the season, but participated in a large successful season at Napoli, who finished in second place in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serie_A" title="201213 Serie A">Serie A that season</a>. Throughout the season, he made 43 appearances, scoring five goals and providing seven assists. The competition for places with players like <a href="/wiki/Edinson_Cavani" title="Edinson Cavani">Edinson Cavani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goran_Pandev" title="Goran Pandev">Goran Pandev</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Vargas" title="Eduardo Vargas">Eduardo Vargas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Omar_El_Kaddouri" title="Omar El Kaddouri">Omar El Kaddouri</a> meant Insigne often started matches on the substitutes' bench.
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_S.S.C._Napoli_season" title="201314 S.S.C. Napoli season">following season</a>, Insigne made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> debut in 21 home win over the previous season's finalists <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, on 18 September 2013. Insigne marked his debut in the competition with a goal from a free-kick.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2014_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2014 Coppa Italia Final">final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Coppa_Italia" title="201314 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> on 3 May 2014, Insigne scored twice in the first half as his side won 31 against <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_S.S.C._Napoli_season" title="201415 S.S.C. Napoli season">201415 season</a>, on 9 November 2014, Insigne injured the <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior cruciate ligament</a> of his right knee in a match against Fiorentina.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He returned to the pitch on 4 April 2015 after a five-month absence, coming on as a substitute in a 10 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In his next league match, on 26 April, he scored a goal in a 42 home win over <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a>, also wearing the captain's armband during the match, in the absence of teammates <a href="/wiki/Marek_Ham%C5%A1%C3%ADk" title="Marek Hamík">Marek Hamík</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Maggio" title="Christian Maggio">Christian Maggio</a> and <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6khan_Inler" title="Gökhan Inler">Gökhan Inler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 September 2015, Insigne opened the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Serie_A" title="201516 Serie A">201516 Serie A season</a> by scoring in a 22 draw against <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On 20 September, he scored again in a 50 win over <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>, also setting up <a href="/wiki/Allan_(footballer,_born_1991)" title="Allan (footballer, born 1991)">Allan</a>'s goal.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 26 September, Insigne made his 100th Serie A appearance with Napoli and scored his third goal of the season in a 21 home win over defending Serie A champions <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> although he was also later forced off the pitch after sustaining an injury during the match; the club, however, later reported the injury was not serious.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He continued his goalscoring run in the following match, scoring twice and setting up Allan's goal in a 40 away win over <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>, bringing his seasonal tally to five goals in seven games.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Insigne's prolific performances even led to comparisons with former Napoli legend <a href="/wiki/Diego_Maradona" title="Diego Maradona">Diego Maradona</a>, which Insigne played down.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2017, Insigne scored his third brace in four appearances for Napoli to take his tally to 14 goals for the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Serie_A" title="201617 Serie A">Serie A season</a>, surpassing his previous personal best for a single campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 October 2017, Insigne scored his 100th career club goal in a 10 away win over <a href="/wiki/Derby_del_Sole" title="Derby del Sole">rivals</a> Roma in Serie A.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Marek_Ham%C5%A1%C3%ADk" title="Marek Hamík">Marek Hamík</a>'s departure from the club in February 2019, Insigne was made the official <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> of Napoli.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On 24 August, in the opening game of the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Serie_A" title="201920 Serie A">201920 Serie A</a> season, Insigne scored twice and set up two more goals in a 43 away win over Fiorentina.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 June 2020, Insigne assisted <a href="/wiki/Dries_Mertens" title="Dries Mertens">Dries Mertens</a>'s equalising goal in a 11 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> in the second leg of <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Coppa_Italia" title="201920 Coppa Italia">201920 Coppa Italia</a> semi-final; the goal saw the Belgian overtake Hamík to become Napoli's outright all-time leading goalscorer with 122 goals, while the result also allowed Napoli to advanced to the <a href="/wiki/2020_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2020 Coppa Italia Final">Coppa Italia Final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In the final on 17 June, Insigne netted Napoli's first spot-kick in a 42 penalty shoot-out victory against Juventus following a 00 draw after regulation time; Insigne went on to lift the trophy as the club's captain.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A regular member of the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy under-21 squad</a>, Insigne made 15 appearances for the "Azzurrini", scoring seven goals. He made his debut with the under-21 side on 6 October 2011 in a <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification">European qualifying</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_under-21_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national under-21 football team">Liechtenstein</a>, scoring two goals and providing two assists in a 72 victory.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the under-21 team, he participated in the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a> under manager <a href="/wiki/Devis_Mangia" title="Devis Mangia">Devis Mangia</a>, playing an important role in Italy's tournament run. On 5 June 2013, he made his tournament debut against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a>, scoring a goal from a free-kick in Italy's 10 opening victory.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On 9 June, in Italy's second match against hosts <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_under-21_football_team" title="Israel national under-21 football team">Israel</a>, Insigne began the play which led to Italy's first goal of the match in the 18th minute, which was scored by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Saponara" title="Riccardo Saponara">Riccardo Saponara</a>. He injured himself later during the match and was forced to come off, although Italy won the match 40.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Insigne was able to recuperate in time for the semi-final match against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands</a>, and he came on to set up <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Borini" title="Fabio Borini">Fabio Borini</a>'s winner which sent the Italians into the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_Final" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship Final">final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On 18 June, Italy was defeated 42 against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spain</a> in the final, although Insigne was able to set up Italy's second goal of the match, which was scored by Borini.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Insigne was called up for the first time for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy senior team</a> in September 2012 at age 21 by coach <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a> for Italy's <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualifying" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualifying">2014 FIFA World Cup qualifying</a> matches against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>. He made his senior debut on 11 September 2012 in the World Cup qualifier match against Malta in <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Modena</a>, coming on as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Diamanti" title="Alessandro Diamanti">Alessandro Diamanti</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On 14 August 2013, Insigne scored his first goal in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, which ended in a 21 loss at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Insigne was named in Cesare Prandelli's 30-man provisional squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a> and was eventually picked in the final 23-man squad.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In Italy's last warm-up match, against <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a> in Brazil ahead of their World Cup opener against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, Insigne and his teammate Ciro Immobile scored five goals, with Insigne scoring two.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 June, Insigne made his debut in the 2014 World Cup in Italy's second group match, against <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Candreva" title="Antonio Candreva">Antonio Candreva</a> in the second half. However, this was Insigne's only appearance in the tournament, as Italy was eliminated in the group stage.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 May 2016, Insigne was named to <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Conte" title="Antonio Conte">Antonio Conte</a>'s 23-man Italy squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He made his first appearance of the tournament on 22 June, coming off the bench in Italy's final group match, which ended in a 10 defeat to the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a>, striking the post and later receiving a yellow card in injury time.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In the round of 16, at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_France" title="Stade de France">Stade de France</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> on 27 June, he came off the bench once again to help set-up <a href="/wiki/Graziano_Pell%C3%A8" title="Graziano Pellè">Graziano Pellè</a>'s 91st minute volley to give the <i>Azzurri</i> a 20 win over defending champions <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> On 2 July, he made a further substitute appearance in the quarter-final fixture against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> and scored Italy's first penalty in the resulting shoot-out, which ended in a 65 loss to the reigning World Cup champions.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nicknamed "<i>Lorenzo Il Magnifico</i>",<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Insigne is a fast, talented, skilful and diminutive right footed <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Winger" title="Forward (association football)">winger</a>, with an eye for goal, who is usually deployed on the left in a <a href="/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#4.E2.80.933.E2.80.933" title="Formation (association football)">433</a> or in a <a href="/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#4.E2.80.932.E2.80.933.E2.80.931" title="Formation (association football)">4231</a> formation, which allows him to cut inside and <a href="/wiki/Curl_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Curl (football)">curl</a> shots on goal with his stronger foot.<sup id="cite_ref-wonderkid_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wonderkid-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne_and_his_hair-raising_rise_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne_and_his_hair-raising_rise-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-World_Cup_Squad_Selector_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-World_Cup_Squad_Selector-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Spurs_in_for_Insigne_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spurs_in_for_Insigne-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Del_Piero_idolo_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Del_Piero_idolo-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Although his preferred role is on the left flank, he is a hard-working, tactically intelligent, and versatile forward, capable of playing in any offensive position on either side of the pitch, or even through the centre; he is also known for his defensive contribution and ability to cover a lot of ground during matches, in addition to his offensive capabilities, despite his lack of physicality.<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Del_Piero_idolo_61-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Del_Piero_idolo-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> He has often operated in deeper, more creative positions, either in a free role in the centre as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a> behind the strikers,<sup id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> or as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Second_striker" title="Forward (association football)">supporting forward</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Spurs_in_for_Insigne_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spurs_in_for_Insigne-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Del_Piero_idolo_61-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Del_Piero_idolo-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> due to his passing ability and vision, which enable him to link-up with midfielders, create chances, and provide <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assist (football)">assists</a> for teammates.<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Euro_2016_Squad_Selector_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Euro_2016_Squad_Selector-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> He is also capable of playing in a more offensive central role as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#False_9" title="Forward (association football)">false 9</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his ability to set-up goals, he is also capable of scoring them himself,<sup id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_55-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> and is an accurate <a href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct free kick">set-piece</a> taker.<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne_and_his_hair-raising_rise_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne_and_his_hair-raising_rise-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Insigne's resulting low centre of gravity, combined with his creativity, quick feet and technical ability, make him extremely quick and agile in possession, and give him excellent balance and control of the ball, which, along with his flair, intelligent movement, speed, and <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribbling</a> skills, allows him to beat opponents and create space for his team in attacking areas, or make attacking runs off the ball into the box.<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wonderkid_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wonderkid-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne_67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Euro_2016_Squad_Selector_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Euro_2016_Squad_Selector-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Best_Young_Players_at_the_2014_World_Cup_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_Young_Players_at_the_2014_World_Cup-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Insigne_just_what_Napoli_need_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Insigne_just_what_Napoli_need-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Napoli_contending_on_three_stages_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Napoli_contending_on_three_stages-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> Regarded as one of Italy's most promising prospects in his youth,<sup id="cite_ref-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lorenzo_Insigne:_1991__Italia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wonderkid_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wonderkid-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coming_of_age_55-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coming_of_age-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne_67-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dear_Lorenzo_Insigne-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Best_Young_Players_at_the_2014_World_Cup_70-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_Young_Players_at_the_2014_World_Cup-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> due to his attributes, skill, pace and small stature, his former Napoli teammate and <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_national_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Macedonia national football team">Macedonia</a> <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> Goran Pandev has referred to him as the "Italian <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Messi</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Manager <a href="/wiki/Delio_Rossi" title="Delio Rossi">Delio Rossi</a> also likened him to compatriots <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Miccoli" title="Fabrizio Miccoli">Fabrizio Miccoli</a> &#160;"for his turn of pace and cleverness"  and <a href="/wiki/Gianfranco_Zola" title="Gianfranco Zola">Gianfranco Zola</a> in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> After scoring a <a href="/wiki/Goal_celebration" title="Goal celebration">goal, Insigne often celebrates</a> by making a "heart" gesture with his hands.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Insigne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
