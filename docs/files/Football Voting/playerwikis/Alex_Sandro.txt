<p><b>Alex Sandro Lobo Silva</b> (born 26 January 1991) is a Brazilian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Left-back" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-back">left-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil national team</a>.
</p><p>At club level, Alex Sandro began his career with <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Paranaense" class="mw-redirect" title="Clube Atlético Paranaense">Atletico Paranaense</a>, and later also played for <a href="/wiki/Santos_FC" title="Santos FC">Santos</a> on <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Loan (sport)">loan</a>. In 2011, he joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a> for <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>9.6&#160;million, alongside teammate and countryman <a href="/wiki/Danilo_(footballer,_born_July_1991)" title="Danilo (footballer, born July 1991)">Danilo</a>, who plays as a right back. He joined Juventus in 2015, winning the <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)#Italy" title="Double (association football)">domestic double</a> in his first three seasons, followed by two more consecutive <a href="/wiki/List_of_Italian_football_champions" title="List of Italian football champions">league titles</a> over the following two years.
</p><p>At international level, Alex Sandro also plays for the Brazil national football team, for which he has gained over 10 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> so far. At youth level, he also represented the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-20_football_team" title="Brazil national under-20 football team">Brazil national under-20 football team</a>, winning both the <a href="/wiki/2011_South_American_Youth_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 South American Youth Championship">South American Youth Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> in 2011, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-23_football_team" title="Brazil national under-23 football team">Brazil national under-23 football team</a>, with which he won a <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mexico_2-1_Brazil:_Olympic_final_match_report_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mexico_2-1_Brazil:_Olympic_final_match_report-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was later also part of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_squads#Brazil" title="2019 Copa América squads">Brazil squad</a> that won the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2019 Copa América">2019 Copa América</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atletico_Paranaense">Atletico Paranaense</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atletico Paranaense">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After winning an impressive amount of honours with the Atletico Paranaense youth setup and a very good performance in the Copa Parana with the U23 side, Alex Sandro earned his first call up to the senior side in October 2008. He played one game in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Brasileiro</a>, taking on <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Internacional" title="Sport Club Internacional">Internacional</a> on 18 October.
</p><p>In 2009, Alex Sandro played a role in Atletico's championship run in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Paranaense" title="Campeonato Paranaense">Campeonato Paranaense</a>, playing in eight matches. He scored his first goal on 25 January against <a href="/wiki/Rio_Branco_Sport_Club" title="Rio Branco Sport Club">Rio Branco</a>, passing the ball to himself around the <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">keeper</a>. As of October 2009, he has played in nine <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Brasileiro</a> matches, playing a total of 269 minutes.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Santos">Santos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Santos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alex Sandro was signed by <a href="/wiki/Santos_FC" title="Santos FC">Santos</a> in 2010 on a two-year <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> deal.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Atletico Paranaense sold him to the investors, using Uruguayan club <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Maldonado" title="Deportivo Maldonado">Deportivo Maldonado</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Third-party_ownership_in_association_football" title="Third-party ownership in association football">proxy</a> to hold the registration rights.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to Atletico Paranaense's 2010 financial report, the club received <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_real" title="Brazilian real">R$</a>1,114,000 from Deportivo Maldonado for transactions of unnamed players.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Porto">Porto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Porto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 23 July 2011, Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a> signed Alex Sandro for €9.6&#160;million from the proxy club Deportivo Maldonado.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CMVM2011_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMVM2011-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He signed a five-year contract with a release clause of €50&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-CMVM2011_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMVM2011-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="2015.E2.80.9316"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–16">2015–16</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–16">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 20 August 2015, Alex Sandro joined Italian <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> for €26&#160;million on a five-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He made his club debut on 12 September 2015 in a 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/A.C._ChievoVerona" title="A.C. ChievoVerona">Chievo</a> in <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Serie_A" title="2015–16 Serie A">Serie A</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Defending champions Juventus endured a difficult start to the season, and were in the bottom half of the table by late October. The club mounted a comeback that started with a last-gasp <a href="/wiki/Derby_della_Mole" title="Derby della Mole">Turin derby</a> win on 31 October, with the Brazilian left-back playing a key role in that upturn.<sup id="cite_ref-Sandro_living_up_to_his_billing_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sandro_living_up_to_his_billing-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 November 2015, Sandro assisted <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Dybala" title="Paulo Dybala">Paulo Dybala</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Cross_(association_football)" title="Cross (association football)">cross</a>, helping Juventus beat <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C.%E2%80%93A.C._Milan_rivalry" title="Juventus F.C.–A.C. Milan rivalry">rival</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> 1–0 in Serie A.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Four days later, on 25 November, he assisted another winning goal, for <a href="/wiki/Mario_Mand%C5%BEuki%C4%87" title="Mario Mandžuki">Mario Mandžuki</a>, in a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League group stage">UEFA Champions League</a> to secure the club a spot in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League_knockout_phase" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League knockout phase">round of 16</a> of the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On 17 January 2016, Alex Sandro scored his first Juventus goal – and his first in Serie A – from the top of the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_area" title="Penalty area">18-yard box</a> in the 42nd minute of a 4–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 February, it was confirmed Alex Sandro would be sidelined for ten days after he picked up an injury to his <a href="/wiki/Rectus_femoris_muscle" title="Rectus femoris muscle">rectus femoris muscle</a> in his left thigh during training at the <a href="/wiki/Juventus_Training_Center_(Vinovo)" title="Juventus Training Center (Vinovo)">Juventus Center</a> the day before, forcing him to miss out on the first leg of the round of 16 of the Champions League on 23 February against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He returned for the second leg in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, where he could not prevent his team's elimination despite putting on a strong individual performance.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Brazilian completed his first season in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> winning a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Italian_football_champions" title="List of Italian football champions">Serie A</a>–<a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Coppa_Italia" title="2015–16 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> double. He came on as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Patrice_Evra" title="Patrice Evra">Patrice Evra</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2016 Coppa Italia Final">Coppa Italia final</a> against Milan at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a> in Rome, which was decided by an <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Morata" title="Álvaro Morata">Álvaro Morata</a> lone goal in <a href="/wiki/Extra_time_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Extra time (association football)">extra time</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17">2016–17</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–17">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Despite scoring an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> in a 3–1 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Alex Sandro carried his form of the previous season into the new one.<sup id="cite_ref-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> A series of impressive performances saw him displace incumbent Patrice Evra from the starting left back role.<sup id="cite_ref-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The Frenchman eventually moved to <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alex Sandro completed the season with 43 appearances across competitions, 11 more than the previous year. That made him one of the most frequently used players by coach <a href="/wiki/Massimiliano_Allegri" title="Massimiliano Allegri">Massimiliano Allegri</a>. From a statistical standpoint, the Brazilian also further improved his goalscoring output, netting three times in the league as Juventus completed a third successive league and cup double.
</p><p>The one trophy that evaded Alex Sandro was the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, which saw his side lose 4–1 in the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2017 UEFA Champions League Final">final</a> to holders <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Stadium" title="Millennium Stadium">Millennium Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>. Alex Sandro played a key role in Mario Mandžuki's temporary equaliser in the first half, supplying the cross that was chested down to the Croatian by <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Higua%C3%ADn" title="Gonzalo Higuaín">Gonzalo Higuaín</a>. In the second half, however, Alex Sandro's and his teammates' performances were less convincing, and one of Alex Sandro's mistakes led to a goal from his former Brazil Under-20 and Porto colleague <a href="/wiki/Casemiro" title="Casemiro">Casemiro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the final he had played a vital role in the <i>Bianconeri</i>'s run, especially in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_UEFA_Champions_League_knockout_phase" title="2016–17 UEFA Champions League knockout phase">quarter-finals</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, where excellent defensive performances helped to neutralise the attacking threat of <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Lionel Messi</a>, and enabled Juventus to keep <a href="/wiki/Shutout" title="Shutout">clean sheets</a> across both legs of the tie.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9318"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–18">2017–18</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017–18">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In his third season with Juventus, Alex Sandro made 39 appearances for Juventus in all competitions, scoring four goals, all of which came in Serie A, from 26 league appearances; he finished the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Juventus_F.C._season" title="2017–18 Juventus F.C. season">2017–18 season</a> by winning a third consecutive domestic double with the club.
</p>
<h4><span id="2018.E2.80.9319"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–19">2018–19</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2018–19">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 23 October 2018, Alex Sandro made his 50th <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> appearance (excluding qualifying rounds) in a 1–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He made his 100th Serie A appearance with Juventus on 30 March 2019, in a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In Juventus's following league match on 2 April, a 2–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>, he made his 150th appearance for the club.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 20 April, he scored the temporary equaliser as Juventus came from behind to defeat <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina%E2%80%93Juventus_F.C._rivalry" title="ACF Fiorentina–Juventus F.C. rivalry">rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> 2–1 at home and win the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Serie_A" title="2018–19 Serie A">Serie A title</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On 24 August 2019, on the opening matchday of the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Serie_A" title="2019–20 Serie A">2019–20 Serie A</a> season, Alex Sandro assisted <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Chiellini" title="Giorgio Chiellini">Giorgio Chiellini</a>'s goal in a 1–0 away win against <a href="/wiki/Parma_Calcio_1913" title="Parma Calcio 1913">Parma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On 18 December, he provided two assists, one for Paulo Dybala and another for <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Ronaldo" title="Cristiano Ronaldo">Cristiano Ronaldo</a>, in a 2–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/UC_Sampdoria" class="mw-redirect" title="UC Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alex Sandro is a part of the golden Atlético Paranaense youth setup, which has produced a great amount of defenders in very little time, with <a href="/wiki/Raul_Guilherme_Martins" title="Raul Guilherme Martins">Raul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manoel_Messias_Silva_Carvalho" title="Manoel Messias Silva Carvalho">Manoel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_Alves" title="Ronaldo Alves">Ronaldo Alves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Augusto_Borret_dos_Santos" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos Augusto Borret dos Santos">Carlão</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Costa_de_Souza" title="Bruno Costa de Souza">Bruno Costa</a> all making themselves known in 2009. After being a part of the Brazil under-18 setup, Alex Sandro was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-20_football_team" title="Brazil national under-20 football team">under-20 side</a> in August 2009, alongside teammates Raul, <a href="/wiki/Renan_Foguinho" title="Renan Foguinho">Renan Foguinho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Pimba" title="Gabriel Pimba">Gabriel Pimba</a>. He made his debut in a friendly match at 18 years of age.
</p><p>He was a member of the teams that won the <a href="/wiki/2011_South_American_Youth_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 South American Youth Championship">2011 South American Youth Championship</a> in Peru,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> in Colombia, also featuring in the final of the tournament against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-20_football_team" title="Portugal national under-20 football team">Portugal</a>, which Brazil won 3–2 in extra-time.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also represented the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-23_football_team" title="Brazil national under-23 football team">Brazil under-23 side</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s tournament">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in London, where he made 3 appearances, including one in the final against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_under-23_football_team" title="Mexico national under-23 football team">Mexico</a>, in which Brazil was defeated 2–1.<sup id="cite_ref-Mexico_2-1_Brazil:_Olympic_final_match_report_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mexico_2-1_Brazil:_Olympic_final_match_report-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alex Sandro made his <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">senior international</a> debut on 10 November 2011 in a 2–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Gabon_national_football_team" title="Gabon national football team">Gabon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first international goal on 12 October 2018, in a 2–0 friendly away win over <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_national_football_team" title="Saudi Arabia national football team">Saudi Arabia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2019, Sandro was included in Brazil's 23-man <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_squads#Brazil" title="2019 Copa América squads">squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2019 Copa América">2019 Copa América</a> on home soil by manager <a href="/wiki/Tite_(football_manager)" title="Tite (football manager)">Tite</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In Brazil's final group match on 22 June, a 5–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a>, Sandro made a substitute appearance, coming on for <a href="/wiki/Filipe_Lu%C3%ADs" title="Filipe Luís">Filipe Luís</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In the quarter-final match against <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> on 27 June, he once again made a substitute appearance, replacing the injured Filipe Luís at half-time; following a 0–0 draw, Brazil advanced to the semi-finals 4–3 on penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Sandro started in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Final" title="2019 Copa América Final">2019 Copa América Final</a> against Peru on 7 July, at the <a href="/wiki/Maracan%C3%A3_Stadium" title="Maracanã Stadium">Maracanã Stadium</a>; the match ended in a 3–1 victory to Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-2019_Copa_América_Final_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019_Copa_América_Final-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg/170px-Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="257" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg/255px-Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg/340px-Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2375" data-file-height="3586" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Valais_Cup_2013_-_OM-FC_Porto_13-07-2013_-_Alex_Sandro.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alex Sandro playing for Porto against <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a> in 2013</div></div></div>
<p>Regarded as one of the best left-backs in the world,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Alex Sandro is a quick, energetic and offensive minded <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a>, who is also a strong tackler and a good reader of the game, courtesy of his speed and anticipation.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He is capable of playing anywhere along the left flank; although mainly a full-back, he has also been used as a <a href="/wiki/Wing-back_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wing-back (association football)">wing-back</a><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and as a <a href="/wiki/Wide_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Wide midfielder">wide midfielder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> He has also occasionally been deployed in a more advanced position, as an <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Winger" title="Forward (association football)">outside forward or offensive winger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He has been described as a player who is a "powerful runner, can <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">beat opponents one-on-one</a> and is an excellent <a href="/wiki/Cross_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross (football)">crosser</a> of the ball".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> His playing position, athleticism, and playing style have drawn comparisons with compatriot and <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>-winner <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Carlos" title="Roberto Carlos">Roberto Carlos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_rise_of_Alex_Sandro-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Sandro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 4 January 2021, Alex Sandro tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">COVID-19</a>, amid its <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Italy" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Italy">pandemic in Italy</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> he recovered by 26 January.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
