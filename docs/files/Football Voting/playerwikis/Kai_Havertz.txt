<p><b>Kai Lukas Havertz</b> (born 11 June 1999) is a German professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as an <a href="/wiki/Attacking_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Attacking midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> or <a href="/wiki/Winger_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Winger (association football)">winger</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany national team</a>.
</p><p>Upon making his debut for <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> in 2016, Havertz became the club's youngest-ever debutant in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, and he became their youngest-ever goalscorer when he scored his first goal the following year. He is also the youngest player to reach the milestones of 50 and 100 league appearances in the German top flight.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Havertz was born in <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a>, North Rhine-Westphalia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_career">Youth career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Youth career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Havertz received his first experience in football at the age of four when he joined amateur club Alemannia Mariadorf, where his grandfather, Richard, was chairman of the club.<sup id="cite_ref-journey_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journey-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, he was signed by <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/Alemannia_Aachen" title="Alemannia Aachen">Alemannia Aachen</a> where he spent only a year in the club's academy before joining Bayer Leverkusen at the age of 11.<sup id="cite_ref-UEFA_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UEFA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the years that followed, he had to overcome the challenges associated with growth spurts and in 2016, after scoring 18 goals for the club's U-17 team, he was awarded the silver U-17 <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Walter_Medal" title="Fritz Walter Medal">Fritz Walter Medal</a> before breaking into Leverkusen's senior team the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-journey_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journey-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bayer_Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bayer Leverkusen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg/170px-Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg/255px-Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg/340px-Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="2400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kai-Havertz-August-2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Havertz with <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> in 2018</div></div></div>
<p>Havertz made his debut for Bayer Leverkusen on 15 October 2016, coming on as a second-half <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Charles_Ar%C3%A1nguiz" title="Charles Aránguiz">Charles Aránguiz</a> in a 2–1 Bundesliga loss to <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>. Upon entering the field of play, he became the club's youngest-ever Bundesliga debutant, at the age of 17 years and 126 days, although his record was broken (by 111 days) by <a href="/wiki/Florian_Wirtz" title="Florian Wirtz">Florian Wirtz</a> in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-UEFA_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UEFA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 17 February 2017, he <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assist (football)">assisted</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Karim_Bellarabi" title="Karim Bellarabi">Karim Bellarabi</a> to score the 50,000th Bundesliga goal.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Four days later, following the suspension of teammate <a href="/wiki/Hakan_%C3%87alhano%C4%9Flu" title="Hakan Çalhanolu">Hakan Çalhanolu</a>, he was handed his first <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> start in the first leg of a round of 16 loss to <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was ruled out of the return leg of the fixture in March, however, as the match clashed with the examinations period at his school.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club on 2 April, netting a later equaliser in a 3–3 draw with <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Upon doing so, Havertz broke another club record to become Leverkusen's youngest-ever goalscorer in the Bundesliga, at the age of 17.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He ultimately made 28 appearances across all competitions and scored four goals, including a brace against <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha BSC</a> on the final day of the season, as Leverkusen ended the campaign in 12th position.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 April 2018, Havertz became the youngest player in the history of the Bundesliga to reach 50 appearances at 18 years and 307 days old, breaking the record previously held by <a href="/wiki/Timo_Werner" title="Timo Werner">Timo Werner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He went on to end his second full campaign with the club with 30 league appearances and three goals to his name as Leverkusen ended the season in fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg/160px-Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg" decoding="async" width="160" height="291" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg/240px-Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg/320px-Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg 2x" data-file-width="440" data-file-height="800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kai_Havertz_2020.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Havertz playing for <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in 2020</div></div></div>
<p>Havertz continued to impress during the following season, despite Leverkusen initially struggling in the league, and by the mid-way point of the campaign was the only player to have started every match for the club, scoring six goals along the way.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 20 September 2018, Havertz scored his first two goals in European competitions in a 3–2 win against <a href="/wiki/PFC_Ludogorets_Razgrad" title="PFC Ludogorets Razgrad">Ludogorets Razgrad</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Europa League">2018–19 UEFA Europa League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On 26 January 2019, he became Leverkusen's youngest-ever <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty</a> scorer when he scored from the spot in a 3–0 league win over Wolfsburg, aged 19 years, seven months and 16 days.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The following month, he became the second youngest-ever player to achieve 75 Bundesliga appearances, behind <a href="/wiki/Julian_Draxler" title="Julian Draxler">Julian Draxler</a>, when he started and scored in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Fortuna_D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Fortuna Düsseldorf">Fortuna Düsseldorf</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 13 April, he scored on his 100th appearance for Leverkusen to help the club to a 1–0 league win over <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>. The goal, his 13th for the campaign, also saw him become the youngest player since Stuttgart's own <a href="/wiki/Horst_K%C3%B6ppel" title="Horst Köppel">Horst Köppel</a> in <a href="/wiki/1967%E2%80%9368_Bundesliga" title="1967–68 Bundesliga">1967–68</a> to score 13 goals in a single league season.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On 5 May, he scored his 15th goal of the campaign during a 6–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a>; a match which for the first time ever saw seven goals scored in the first half of a Bundesliga match.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On the final day of the season, he became the highest scoring teenager in a single Bundesliga campaign when he scored his 17th goal during a 5–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha BSC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the season, he was named runner-up to <a href="/wiki/Marco_Reus" title="Marco Reus">Marco Reus</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Footballer_of_the_Year_(Germany)" title="Footballer of the Year (Germany)">German Footballer of the Year</a> award, losing out by just 37 votes.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Bundesliga" title="2019–20 Bundesliga">2019–20 campaign</a>, Havertz scored in Leverkusen's 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/SC_Paderborn_07" title="SC Paderborn 07">Paderborn</a>, becoming the second-youngest player of all time behind Köppel to score 25 Bundesliga goals.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In December, at the age of 20 years, six months and four days, he broke another of Werner's records to become the youngest-ever player to reach 100 Bundesliga appearances when he started in his team's 2–0 defeat to Köln.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2019–20 UEFA Europa League">2019–20 UEFA Europa League</a>, Havertz scored in both matches against <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">Porto</a> in the round of 32, then he scored a goal in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a> in the quarter-final.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea">Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 September 2020, Havertz signed a five-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The transfer fee was reported to be worth an initial £62&#160;million, which could rise to £71&#160;million with add-ons, making him Chelsea's <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chelsea_F.C._records_and_statistics#Highest_transfer_fees_paid" title="List of Chelsea F.C. records and statistics">second-most expensive signing</a> after <a href="/wiki/Kepa_Arrizabalaga" title="Kepa Arrizabalaga">Kepa Arrizabalaga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 14 September, he made his debut with Chelsea in the <a href="/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_Premier_League" title="2020–21 Premier League">league opener</a> against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>, which ended in a 3–1 away win.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On 23 September, Havertz scored his first career hat-trick and his first Chelsea goals in a 6–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> in the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_EFL_Cup" title="2020–21 EFL Cup">EFL Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Havertz scored his first-ever Premier League goal against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> on 17 October, in a 3–3 draw at home.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On 4 November 2020, it was revealed that Havertz had tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">COVID-19</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball,_M%C3%A4nner,_L%C3%A4nderspiel,_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg/170px-2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg/255px-2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg/340px-2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball%2C_M%C3%A4nner%2C_L%C3%A4nderspiel%2C_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3873" data-file-height="2582" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2019-06-11_Fu%C3%9Fball,_M%C3%A4nner,_L%C3%A4nderspiel,_Deutschland-Estland_StP_2058_LR10_by_Stepro.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Havertz with <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> in 2019</div></div></div>
<p>Havertz made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-16_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Germany national under-16 football team">Germany national under-16 team</a> on 11 November 2014, starting in the friendly match against the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_under-16_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Czech Republic national under-16 football team">Czech Republic</a> before being substituted out in the 57th minute for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Baack" title="Tom Baack">Tom Baack</a>. The match finished as a 3–1 win for Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Havertz was included in <a href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_European_Under-17_Championship_squads#Germany" title="2016 UEFA European Under-17 Championship squads">Germany's squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_European_Under-17_Championship" title="2016 UEFA European Under-17 Championship">2016 UEFA European Under-17 Championship</a> in Azerbaijan.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He appeared in all five of Germany's matches, scoring once before Germany were eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-17_football_team" title="Spain national under-17 football team">Spain</a> in the semi-final.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Following a 15-month absence from youth internationals, Havertz made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-19_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Germany national under-19 football team">Germany's under-19 team</a>, debuting on 31 August 2017 in the 0–0 friendly draw against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-19_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-19 football team">Switzerland</a>, coming on in the 72nd minute for <a href="/wiki/Palk%C3%B3_D%C3%A1rdai" title="Palkó Dárdai">Palkó Dárdai</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On 4 October 2017, in his third appearance for the under-19 team, Havertz scored four goals in a 5–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Belarus_national_football_team" title="Belarus national football team">Belarus</a> in the first round of <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship_qualification" title="2018 UEFA European Under-19 Championship qualification">European Under-19 Championship qualifying</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He was later named captain of the under-19 team.<sup id="cite_ref-fcu_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcu-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 August 2018, Havertz was called up to <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany's senior team</a> for the first time by head coach <a href="/wiki/Joachim_L%C3%B6w" title="Joachim Löw">Joachim Löw</a>. He was included in the squad for Germany's <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Nations_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Nations League">UEFA Nations League</a> match against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> and friendly against <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fcu_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcu-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Havertz made his international debut on 9 September 2018, coming on as a substitute in the 88th minute for <a href="/wiki/Timo_Werner" title="Timo Werner">Timo Werner</a> against Peru, with the match finishing as a 2–1 home win for Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Upon making his debut, he became the first player born in 1999 to represent the national team.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kai_Havertz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Havertz has been described as technically gifted, two-footed midfielder who is comfortable with the ball on either foot and adept with his head.<sup id="cite_ref-desc_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-desc-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> During his formative years, his style of play drew early comparisons to compatriot <a href="/wiki/Mesut_%C3%96zil" title="Mesut Özil">Mesut Özil</a>, with Havertz himself admitting the <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> midfielder was a player he looked up to.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> By the age of 19, and following numerous impressive performances in the Bundesliga, further comparisons had been drawn between Havertz and former Leverkusen players such as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ballack" title="Michael Ballack">Michael Ballack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toni_Kroos" title="Toni Kroos">Toni Kroos</a> and some began describing him as a combination of all three and an <i>Alleskönner</i> – a player who can do everything.<sup id="cite_ref-desc_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-desc-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
