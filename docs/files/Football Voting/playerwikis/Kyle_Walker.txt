<p><b>Kyle Andrew Walker</b> (born 28 May 1990) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Right-back" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-back">right-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> and the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Walker was born in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, South Yorkshire,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and is of Jamaican descent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Sharrow" title="Sharrow">Sharrow</a> area of the city,<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph-ambassador_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-ambassador-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and attended Porter Croft Infant &amp; Junior School, followed by <a href="/wiki/High_Storrs_School" title="High Storrs School">High Storrs School</a> until 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
He has three children with Annie Kilner, and a fourth with Lauryn Goodman, sister of model <a href="/wiki/Chloe_Goodman" title="Chloe Goodman">Chloe Goodman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sheffield_United">Sheffield United</span></h3>
<p>Walker joined boyhood club <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-es_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-es-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> at the age of seven after being recommended by coach Paul Archer at <a href="/wiki/Football_Unites,_Racism_Divides" title="Football Unites, Racism Divides">Football Unites, Racism Divides</a> and progressed through the ranks to become a regular fixture in the reserves by 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph-ambassador_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-ambassador-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In November 2008, he was allowed to join <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_One" title="EFL League One">League One</a> club <a href="/wiki/Northampton_Town_F.C." title="Northampton Town F.C.">Northampton Town</a> on a one-month <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> to gain first team experience,<sup id="cite_ref-Northampton_loan_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Northampton_loan-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> making his debut on 15 November 2008 in a defeat by <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Northampton_debut_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Northampton_debut-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His one-month loan was later extended into January,<sup id="cite_ref-Northampton_loan_extended_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Northampton_loan_extended-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> before he eventually returned to Bramall Lane having played nine matches for the Cobblers.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after returning to his parent club, Walker made his full debut for Sheffield United on 13 January 2009, starting in a third round <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> tie against <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blades_debut_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blades_debut-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> With the Blades losing a number of players to injury in the closing weeks of the season, Walker was a surprise inclusion in the starting line up for the crucial last two matches of the season, making his full league debut for the club on 25 April 2009 against <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blades_league_debut_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blades_league_debut-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> After two outstanding performances he retained his place as Sheffield United entered the play–offs, starting both matches against <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> and the final against <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> at the end of May.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> By playing in the final, Walker became the youngest Sheffield United player ever to play at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wembley_Record_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wembley_Record-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2009, Walker left Sheffield United to join <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> along with fellow defender <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Naughton" title="Kyle Naughton">Kyle Naughton</a> for a combined fee of £9&#160;million, but was loaned back to United for the duration of the 2009–10 season as part of the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-spurs_transfer_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spurs_transfer-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> While at <a href="/wiki/Bramall_Lane" title="Bramall Lane">Bramall Lane</a> he was virtually ever present at right-back for the first half of the season but was unexpectedly recalled back to Spurs on 1 February 2010, just before the close of the January 2010 <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer window</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Alan_Hutton" title="Alan Hutton">Alan Hutton</a> was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Walker made his debut for Tottenham on Saturday 27 March 2010, in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> and went on to make one more league appearance that season.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg/170px-Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="209" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg/255px-Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg/340px-Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="846" data-file-height="1041" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kyle_Walker_Spurs_USA_tour_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Walker with <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the following season, Walker went out on loan once more signing an initial six-month deal with <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> (QPR) due to injuries at the club in the right-back position.<sup id="cite_ref-qpr_loan_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qpr_loan-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In mid-October, his loan was extended until 3 January 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-extended_loan_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-extended_loan-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After he had left QPR, Walker joined <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> on loan until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Premier_League" title="2010–11 Premier League">2010–11</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-villa_loan_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villa_loan-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He scored on his debut only nine minutes into the match against former club Sheffield United in the third round of the FA Cup in January 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-fa_cup_goal_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fa_cup_goal-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the start of February, Walker scored his first Premier League goal and first senior league goal, a 30-yard strike low into the left corner against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prem_goal_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prem_goal-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He returned to his parent club at the end of the 2010–11 season after scoring two goals in 18 appearances in all competitions for Aston Villa.
</p><p>On leaving Villa Park, Walker suggested that he would be happy to return to the club the following season,<sup id="cite_ref-villa_return_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villa_return-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> as first team opportunities were limited<sup id="cite_ref-spurs_options_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spurs_options-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> but Tottenham Hotspur manager <a href="/wiki/Harry_Redknapp" title="Harry Redknapp">Harry Redknapp</a> later stated that he did not wish to sell the defender.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Walker subsequently agreed to a contract extension at Tottenham until 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Walker was chosen in the starting eleven for Tottenham's opening match of the 2011–12 season, against Manchester United at Old Trafford on 22 August 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On 2 October, he scored the winning goal in the <a href="/wiki/North_London_derby" title="North London derby">North London derby</a> against Arsenal from 25-yards out. Tottenham won the match 2–1.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 22 April 2012, Walker was named as the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Young_Player_of_the_Year" title="PFA Young Player of the Year">PFA Young Player of the Year</a>, beating the likes of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Ag%C3%BCero" title="Sergio Agüero">Sergio Agüero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Welbeck" title="Danny Welbeck">Danny Welbeck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sturridge" title="Daniel Sturridge">Daniel Sturridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Oxlade-Chamberlain" title="Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain">Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain</a> and teammate <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Bale" title="Gareth Bale">Gareth Bale</a> to the award, as well as being named in the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Team of the Year">PFA Team of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PFA_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PFA-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 29 April, he scored a free kick from 25 yards to help Tottenham beat <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> 2–0 at <a href="/wiki/White_Hart_Lane" title="White Hart Lane">White Hart Lane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On 3 May 2012, Walker was awarded with a new five-year contract, that runs until 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Walker's performance against Manchester United in March 2012 saw him at fault for two of the opposition's goals in a 3–1 defeat.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He began his next season in a similar vein; he was at fault for Chelsea's 4th goal in the 4–2 defeat at White Hart Lane, getting shrugged off the ball easily by Juan Mata.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He was ever-present in the first half of the season in the Premier League, with the exception of the 3–0 victory over Fulham at Craven Cottage; this included playing 90 minutes in the 3–2 win over Manchester United, which was Tottenham's first victory at Old Trafford in 23 years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg/170px-Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="275" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg/255px-Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg/340px-Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="435" data-file-height="703" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kyle_Walker_01-09-2013_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Walker playing for Tottenham Hotspur in 2013</div></div></div>
<p>On 28 October 2013, it was announced that Walker had signed a new contract with Tottenham, keeping him at the club until 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Walker was part of the team that lost 2–0 to <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> at Wembley Stadium in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2015 Football League Cup Final">2015 League Cup Final</a> on 1 March.<sup id="cite_ref-2015LC_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2015LC-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 August 2015, Walker was involved in an own goal which led to Tottenham suffering a defeat in the hands of Manchester United in the season opener and it also became the first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2015-16_Premier_League" class="mw-redirect" title="2015-16 Premier League">new season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 April 2017, Walker was named as right-back in the PFA Team of the Year for the second time.<sup id="cite_ref-PFA1617_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PFA1617-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span></h3>
<p>On 14 July 2017, Walker signed a five-year contract with Tottenham's Premier League rivals <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> in a transfer reported to be worth an initial £45&#160;million fee, rising to £50&#160;million with add-ons, making it one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_expensive_association_football_transfers" title="List of most expensive association football transfers">most expensive football transfers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made his competitive debut for City against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton</a> on 12 August 2017, a game where many media outlets labelled him as the best player.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> However, during Walker's home debut for Manchester City he was sent off for two yellow card offences against Everton, which was thought to be a harsh decision by referee <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Madley" title="Bobby Madley">Bobby Madley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Walker was selected and part of the squad that beat Arsenal 3–0 at Wembley Stadium in the <a href="/wiki/2018_EFL_Cup_Final" title="2018 EFL Cup Final">2018 EFL Cup Final</a> to claim his and City's first trophy of the 2017–18 season.<sup id="cite_ref-2018LC_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018LC-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Walker signed a new contract with the club in June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 November 2019, in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> away match against <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a>, Walker <a href="/wiki/List_of_outfield_association_footballers_who_played_in_goal" title="List of outfield association footballers who played in goal">volunteered to go in goal</a> for the last 10 minutes after substitute goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Bravo" title="Claudio Bravo">Claudio Bravo</a> was sent off; he did not concede as the match ended 1–1.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 April 2020, the club began a disciplinary procedure against Walker after it was reported that he had broken national lockdown rules and invited two sex workers to his home amid the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">global coronavirus pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On 7 May 2020, it was again reported that he had broken lockdown rules by travelling to Sheffield to give a present to his sister and hugging her. He later admitted that he also visited his parents to pick up food. Manchester City said they would not discipline him for this, citing the trip's being for personal reasons. He later tweeted that he felt he and his family were being harassed and that the reports were affecting the mental health of his whole family.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span></h2>
<p>Before having even made a league appearance for Sheffield United, Walker was called up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">England U19s</a> in February 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-england_call_up_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england_call_up-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He made his début on 10 February, coming on as a second-half substitute in a 3–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-19_football_team" title="Spain national under-19 football team">Spain U19s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-england_début_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england_début-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Following his first run of regular Premier League starts, Walker was called up to the full <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> squad in February 2011 and again in March 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> However, he did not feature in any of the matches played and pulled out of the squad in March due to an injury.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>At the end of the 2010–11 season, Walker was named as part of the England squad that competed in the <a href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a> competition in Denmark.<sup id="cite_ref-england_u21_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england_u21-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Despite the poor showing of the team who were knocked out in the first round, Walker was named in the Team of the Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-TOTT_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOTT-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His good form in the 2011–12 season saw him make his senior England début in the 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>, coming on for <a href="/wiki/Scott_Parker" title="Scott Parker">Scott Parker</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in the 85th minute.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc15693231_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc15693231-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He made his first start on 15 November, in a 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>, after which he was the man-of-the-match.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> He missed the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">2012 European Championships</a> because of a toe injury, suffered in a league match against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Walker played the first England match after Euro 2012, a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was named in the 23-man <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national team</a> squad for the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Walker was deployed as a centre-back in a back three by England's manager <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Southgate" title="Gareth Southgate">Gareth Southgate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, he scored an own goal against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a> as England went out at the semi-final stage of the <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Nations_League_Finals" title="2019 UEFA Nations League Finals">2019 UEFA Nations League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> He was not selected for England's squad for the European Championship qualifiers at the start of the 2019–20 season.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On 5 September 2020, he was sent off in a 1–0 Nations League win away to <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland</a>, the first Englishman to get an international red card since <a href="/wiki/Raheem_Sterling" title="Raheem Sterling">Raheem Sterling</a> in June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorship">Sponsorship</span></h2>
<p>In December 2012, Walker signed a boot deal with <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a> to wear the T90 Laser IV which were also worn by <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> From 2009 to 2012, he wore the SX Valor, SX Flare, Stealth Pro, Speciali, Stealth Pro II, GT Pro, and GT Pro II boots by <a href="/wiki/Umbro" title="Umbro">Umbro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
