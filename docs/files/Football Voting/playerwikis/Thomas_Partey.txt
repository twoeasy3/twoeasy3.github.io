<p><b>Thomas Teye Partey</b> (born 13 June 1993) is a Ghanaian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and is the vice-captain of the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national team</a>.
</p><p>Partey began his professional career at Spanish club <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> in 2013, going on loan to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">Mallorca</a> and <a href="/wiki/UD_Almer%C3%ADa" title="UD Almería">Almería</a>, and returned to Atlético in 2015, with whom he won the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Super_Cup" title="UEFA Super Cup">UEFA Super Cup</a> in 2018. In 2020, he joined Arsenal in a <a href="/wiki/Transfer_(association_football)" title="Transfer (association football)">transfer</a> worth £45 million (€50 million), becoming <a href="/wiki/List_of_most_expensive_association_football_transfers" title="List of most expensive association football transfers">the most expensive Ghanaian player</a> of all time.
</p><p>A Ghanaian international, Partey represented his nation at two <a href="/wiki/Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="Africa Cup of Nations">Africa Cup of Nations</a> tournaments. He was named into the <a href="/wiki/CAF_Team_of_the_Year" title="CAF Team of the Year">CAF Team of the Year</a> in 2018, and won <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Player_of_the_Year" title="Ghana Player of the Year">Ghana Player of the Year</a> in 2018 and 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlético_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Thomas_Partey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Thomas_Partey.jpg/150px-Thomas_Partey.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Thomas_Partey.jpg/225px-Thomas_Partey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Thomas_Partey.jpg/300px-Thomas_Partey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="576" data-file-height="979" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Thomas_Partey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Partey with <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> in 2018</div></div></div>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Krobo_Odumase" title="Krobo Odumase">Krobo Odumase</a>, Partey was a product of local Odometah's youth ranks.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He signed with <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> in 2011, and was subsequently moved to <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid_B" title="Atlético Madrid B">the reserves</a> a year later. On 10 March 2013, Partey was called up to the main squad for the match against <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, he remained unused in the eventual 0–1 home defeat.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Mallorca_and_Almer.C3.ADa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mallorca_and_Almería">Mallorca and Almería</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mallorca and Almería">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 12 July, Partey was loaned to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">Mallorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_La_Liga" title="2012–13 La Liga">freshly relegated to the second level</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On 18 August, he made his professional debut, in a 0–4 away defeat against <a href="/wiki/CE_Sabadell_FC" title="CE Sabadell FC">Sabadell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Partey scored his first professional goal on 15 September, netting his side's second of a 2–2 draw at <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9rcules_CF" title="Hércules CF">Hércules</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 27 July 2014, Partey joined <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/UD_Almer%C3%ADa" title="UD Almería">Almería</a> on a loan.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut in the competition on 23 August, starting in a 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Partey scored his first goals in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_football_league_system" title="Spanish football league system">the main category of Spanish football</a> on 11 April 2015, netting a brace in a 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Granada_CF" title="Granada CF">Granada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Return_to_Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Atlético_Madrid">Return to Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Partey made his first team debut for Atlético Madrid on 28 November 2015, replacing <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Vietto" title="Luciano Vietto">Luciano Vietto</a> in a 1–0 home win against Espanyol.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 2 January of the following year, he scored his first league goal for the club, netting the game's only strike in a home success over <a href="/wiki/Levante_UD" title="Levante UD">Levante</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On 28 May, he played in the <a href="/wiki/2016_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2016 UEFA Champions League Final">UEFA Champions League Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Koke_(footballer,_born_1992)" title="Koke (footballer, born 1992)">Koke</a> in the 116th minute as his side lost on penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-final_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-final-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Partey signed a contract extension with Atlético Madrid through 2022 on 14 February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 31 October, he scored his first European goal with a long-range strike to equalise at home to <a href="/wiki/FK_Qarabag" class="mw-redirect" title="FK Qarabag">Qarabag</a> in a 1–1 draw in the Champions League group game; he became the first African to score in the competition for Atlético.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Following his impressive performances for the club, he was rewarded with another contract on 1 March 2018, this time until 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 16 May, he played in the <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2018 UEFA Europa League Final">2018 UEFA Europa League Final</a>, as his side won 3–0 against <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-europa_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 September 2019, Partey came on as a late substitute and netted the match's winner in the last minute of the game, as Atlético came back from 2–0 down to win the game by 3–2 against <a href="/wiki/SD_Eibar" title="SD Eibar">Eibar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He marked his 100th <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> appearance for <i>Los Rojiblancos</i> with a <a href="/wiki/Player_of_the_match" title="Player of the match">man-of-the-match</a> performance in a 0–0 draw against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Derby" title="Madrid Derby">Madrid Derby</a> four weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arsenal">Arsenal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Arsenal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 October 2020, <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> announced the signing of Partey on a long-term contract, after activating his £45 million (€50 million) <a href="/wiki/Buyout_clause" title="Buyout clause">release clause</a> with <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>. He was given the number 18 shirt, which had been vacated by <a href="/wiki/Nacho_Monreal" title="Nacho Monreal">Nacho Monreal</a> the previous season.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Upon signing, Partey stated his desire to help Arsenal "back where [they] belong", describing his decision to move being based on "[wanting] to experience new challenges", while also crediting the transfer to manager <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Arteta" title="Mikel Arteta">Mikel Arteta</a> and technical director <a href="/wiki/Edu_Gaspar" title="Edu Gaspar">Edu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 17 October 2020, Partey made his debut for Arsenal as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Granit_Xhaka" title="Granit Xhaka">Granit Xhaka</a> in a 0–1 away defeat in the league against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Five days later, Partey started his first match for Arsenal in a 2–1 away win over <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">Rapid Wien</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Midway through a match against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> on 8 November, Partey suffered a thigh injury which saw him miss the rest of the month's games.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Partey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2016, Partey was called up for the first time to the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national team</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Avram_Grant" title="Avram Grant">Avram Grant</a>, ahead of a <a href="/wiki/2017_Africa_Cup_of_Nations_qualification" title="2017 Africa Cup of Nations qualification">2017 Africa Cup of Nations qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Mauritius_national_football_team" title="Mauritius national football team">Mauritius</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut on 5 June, replacing <a href="/wiki/Frank_Acheampong" title="Frank Acheampong">Frank Acheampong</a> for the final 11 minutes of a 2–0 away win that booked the Black Stars' position in the <a href="/wiki/2017_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2017 Africa Cup of Nations">finals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 5 September 2017, Partey scored his first international hat-trick in a 5–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Congo_national_football_team" title="Congo national football team">Congo</a> in <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_Qualification" class="mw-redirect" title="2018 FIFA World Cup Qualification">2018 FIFA World Cup Qualification</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Partey was chosen in <a href="/wiki/James_Kwesi_Appiah" title="James Kwesi Appiah">Kwesi Appiah</a>'s 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2019_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2019 Africa Cup of Nations">2019 Africa Cup of Nations</a> in Egypt.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In their last group game, he scored in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau_national_football_team" title="Guinea-Bissau national football team">Guinea-Bissau</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Suez_Stadium" title="Suez Stadium">Suez Stadium</a> as the Black Stars topped their group.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He netted in the penalty shootout at the end of the last-16 game against <a href="/wiki/Tunisia_national_football_team" title="Tunisia national football team">Tunisia</a> on 8 July, though his team was eliminated.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Partey won <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Player_of_the_Year" title="Ghana Player of the Year">Ghana Player of the Year</a> in 2018 and 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-ghana18_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ghana18-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ghana19_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ghana19-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2021_Africa_Cup_of_Nations_qualification" title="2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualification">2021 Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers</a>, as well for qualifiers for the <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022 FIFA World Cup</a>, Partey was named Ghana's vice-captain.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
