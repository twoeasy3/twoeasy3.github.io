<p><b>Onyinye Wilfred Ndidi</b> (born 16 December 1996) is a Nigerian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria national team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>. He is known for his defensive prowess and long range strikes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genk">Genk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Genk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ndidi made his <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian Pro League</a> debut with <a href="/wiki/K.R.C._Genk" title="K.R.C. Genk">Genk</a> on 31 January 2015 against <a href="/wiki/R._Charleroi_S.C." title="R. Charleroi S.C.">Charleroi</a> in a 1–0 away defeat. He played the first 74 minutes of the game, before being substituted for <a href="/wiki/Jarne_Vrijsen" title="Jarne Vrijsen">Jarne Vrijsen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-soccerway2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soccerway2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Belgian League play-off game against <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_KV" title="Club Brugge KV">Club Brugge</a>, Ndidi scored a long-range goal, which was named the goal of the season in the Belgian league. After receiving an attempted-clearance outside the penalty box, he lofted the ball smoothly over an opposing player before unleashing a ferocious volley into the top right corner.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The ball was adjudged to be travelling at over 111&#160;km/h.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leicester_City">Leicester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Leicester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 December 2016, Genk agreed a £17 million transfer deal with <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The deal was confirmed on 5 January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ndidi made his debut for the club on 7 January 2017, in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in the third round of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He made his first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> start on 14 January 2017 against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> at home in a 3–0 defeat.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the English FA Cup game against Derby County on 8 February 2017, Ndidi came on in the first half of extra time and scored his first goal for Leicester through a long-range shot.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on 27 February 2017, Ndidi won 11 of his 14 tackles,<sup id="cite_ref-Leicester_City_3-1_Liverpool_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_City_3-1_Liverpool-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> a feat bettered only by Chelsea's <a href="/wiki/N%27Golo_Kant%C3%A9" title="N&#39;Golo Kanté">N'Golo Kanté</a> who made 14 tackles against the same club in January.<sup id="cite_ref-Leicester_dynamo_Wilfred_Ndidi_has_potential_to_surpass_N&#39;Golo_Kante_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_dynamo_Wilfred_Ndidi_has_potential_to_surpass_N&#39;Golo_Kante-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ndidi was sent off for the first time in his career during Leicester's 3–0 home defeat to Crystal Palace on 16 December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 2019–20 season,he scored the opener of the season for Leicester against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2020-21_season">2020-21 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2020-21 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 13 September 2020, Ndidi started at centre-back in Leicester City's opening game of the <a href="/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_Premier_League" title="2020–21 Premier League">2020–21 Premier League</a> campaign and kept a clean sheet in a 3–0 win away to <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>. Ndidi suffered an adductor injury on 20 September 2020 and was ruled out for 6–12 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He returned to action on 3 December in Leicester's Europa League loss to <a href="/wiki/FC_Zorya_Luhansk" title="FC Zorya Luhansk">Zorya Luhansk</a>.
</p><p>On 19 January 2021, Ndidi scored his first goal of the season in Leicester City's Premier League fixture against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>. The match eventually ended with Leicester beating their opponents 2–0.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wilfred_Ndidi-Nigeria.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Wilfred_Ndidi-Nigeria.jpg/220px-Wilfred_Ndidi-Nigeria.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fb/Wilfred_Ndidi-Nigeria.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="246" data-file-height="265" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wilfred_Ndidi-Nigeria.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ndidi with Nigeria against Argentina</div></div></div>
<p>Ndidi was part of the Nigerian youth setup during his time at Nathaniel Boys of Lagos. While playing the African U-17 Championship with Nigeria, he was excluded along with two other players from the competition as a precaution, following an MRI age test that suggested he was just slightly above the threshold.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Notwithstanding, he joined up with his teammates in the U-20 team the following year, forming the bedrock of the midfield. He was called up to the Senior <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria national football team</a> on 8 October 2015, making his debut in the friendly game against DR Congo, and playing again a few days later in the 3–0 win against Cameroon, when he replaced <a href="/wiki/John_Obi_Mikel" title="John Obi Mikel">John Obi Mikel</a> in the 63rd minute.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was selected by <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> for their 35-man provisional squad for the 2016 Summer Olympics.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018 he was named in Nigeria’s preliminary 30-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2018_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2018 World Cup">2018 World Cup</a> in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He was included in the <a href="/wiki/African_Cup_of_Nations_2019" class="mw-redirect" title="African Cup of Nations 2019">African Cup of Nations 2019</a> squad<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and played in Nigeria's first match against <a href="/wiki/Burundi_national_football_team" title="Burundi national football team">Burundi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Ndidi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2019, Ndidi married his longtime girlfriend Dinma Fortune in <a href="/wiki/Abuja" title="Abuja">Abuja</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Ndidi started studying for a degree in Business and Management at <a href="/wiki/De_Montfort_University" title="De Montfort University">De Montfort University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
