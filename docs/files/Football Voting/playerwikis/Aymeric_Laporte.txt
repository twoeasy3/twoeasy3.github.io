<p><b>Aymeric Jean Louis Gérard Alphonse Laporte</b> (<small>French pronunciation:&#160;&#8203;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[mik lapt]</a></span>; born 27 May 1994) is a French professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Centre-back" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre-back">centre-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.
</p><p>After joining <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> in 2010 at the age of 16, he became only the second French player—after <a href="/wiki/Bixente_Lizarazu" title="Bixente Lizarazu">Bixente Lizarazu</a>—to play for them,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> going on to make 220 competitive appearances for the club and score ten goals. In January 2018, he signed for Manchester City.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was part of the side which won the first <a href="/wiki/Treble_(association_football)" title="Treble (association football)">domestic treble</a> in English men’s football in 2019.
</p><p>Laporte won 51 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for <a href="/wiki/France_national_youth_football_team" title="France national youth football team">France at youth level</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletic_Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Athletic Bilbao">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Laporte was born in <a href="/wiki/Agen" title="Agen">Agen</a>, Aquitaine and is of <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basque</a> descent through his great-grandparents.<sup id="cite_ref-Blood_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blood-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He started playing football and <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> at the age of five, and began his development at <a href="/wiki/SU_Agen_Football" title="SU Agen Football">SU Agen Football</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Among_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Among-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bio_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2009 he was invited to join <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a> after being scouted;<sup id="cite_ref-Among_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Among-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bio_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> by arrangement with the club, he spent a season with <a href="/wiki/Aviron_Bayonnais_FC" title="Aviron Bayonnais FC">Aviron Bayonnais FC</a><sup id="cite_ref-Bio_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Butterflies_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butterflies-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> as he was too young to move outside France at the time.
</p><p>Laporte arrived in <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a> formally in 2010, joining Athletic's youth setup at <a href="/wiki/Lezama_Facilities" title="Lezama Facilities">Lezama</a>, prompting debate locally over whether his signing met the criteria of <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao_signing_policy" title="Athletic Bilbao signing policy">their policy</a> due to his tenuous links to the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">Basque region</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blood_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blood-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Among_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Among-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Butterflies_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butterflies-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He went on to play for the club's <a href="/wiki/CD_Basconia" title="CD Basconia">farm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bilbao_Athletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilbao Athletic">reserve teams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 28 November 2012 he was given his debut with the main squad by manager <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Bielsa" title="Marcelo Bielsa">Marcelo Bielsa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 away win against <a href="/wiki/Hapoel_Ironi_Kiryat_Shmona_F.C." title="Hapoel Ironi Kiryat Shmona F.C.">Hapoel Ironi Kiryat Shmona F.C.</a> in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Europa League">that season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Laporte made his first <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> appearance on 9 December 2012, appearing one minute in a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Celta_de_Vigo" class="mw-redirect" title="Celta de Vigo">Celta de Vigo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was inserted in the starting XI the following week, helping the <i>Lions</i> to the same result at <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and was definitely promoted to the first team shortly after, signing a new contract until 2015;<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> on 14 January 2013, he was given the number 4 jersey previously worn by <a href="/wiki/Ustaritz_Aldekoaotalora" title="Ustaritz Aldekoaotalora">Ustaritz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and late in the month he renewed his contract, running until 2016 and with a €27.5&#160;million release clause.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Laporte became an undisputed starter under new coach <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Valverde" title="Ernesto Valverde">Ernesto Valverde</a>, also being deployed as <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> on occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal as a professional on 28 October 2013, netting the game's only at <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> At the end of <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_La_Liga" title="2013–14 La Liga">the campaign</a>, he was voted into the <a href="/wiki/LFP_Awards#Team_of_the_Year" class="mw-redirect" title="LFP Awards">La Liga Team of the Year</a> by the reporters of the <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_F%C3%BAtbol_Profesional" class="mw-redirect" title="Liga de Fútbol Profesional">Liga de Fútbol Profesional</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2014a_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014a-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2014b_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014b-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2015, Laporte extended his link until 2019 with a release clause of €50&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_del_Rey_Final" title="2015 Copa del Rey Final">losing the final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> to <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> he played both legs of <a href="/wiki/2015_Supercopa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="2015 Supercopa de España">the year</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Supercopa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Supercopa de España">Supercopa de España</a> triumph against the same opposition, Athletic's first silverware for 31 years.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SUPERCUP_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SUPERCUP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 June 2016, Laporte renewed his contract until 2020, with his buyout clause rising to an initial €65&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manchester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Near the end of the winter transfer window of January 2018, Laporte signed for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> for a reported fee of £57&#160;million (his contractual <a href="/wiki/Release_clause" class="mw-redirect" title="Release clause">release clause</a> amount and a record-breaking figure for both clubs). He was given the number 14 shirt,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> while Athletic immediately spent around half of the fee on <a href="/wiki/I%C3%B1igo_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Iñigo Martínez">Iñigo Martínez</a> as a replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9318_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–18_season">2017–18 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2017–18 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Just one day after signing, Laporte made his Manchester City and <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>. City came out victorious winning 3–0 as they kept up their title charge.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He went on to make 13 appearances throughout the season for City, helping them accumulate an unprecedented 100 points in a Premier League season. He made a total of nine league appearances meaning he was eligible for a winner's medal.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2018.E2.80.9319_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–19_season">2018–19 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2018–19 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Laporte scored his first goal for City on 25 August 2018, equalising against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> in a 1–1 away draw.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He made 51 appearances during the 2018–19 season as the club completed the first domestic <a href="/wiki/Treble_(association_football)" title="Treble (association football)">treble</a> in English football history (<a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Premier_League" title="2018–19 Premier League">Premier League</a>, <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_FA_Cup" title="2018–19 FA Cup">FA Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_EFL_Cup" title="2018–19 EFL Cup">League Cup</a>, plus the <a href="/wiki/2018_FA_Community_Shield" title="2018 FA Community Shield">Community Shield</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-facup19_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facup19-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He scored five goals throughout the season, including an important goal on the final day of the Premier League campaign as City romped to victory over <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton and Hove Albion</a> securing back-to-back league titles and the second of Laporte's career. Overall he played a total of 4,352 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-sb1819_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sb1819-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2019.E2.80.9320_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–20_season">2019–20 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2019–20 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 31 August 2019, Laporte sustained an injury to his right knee, causing him to be  carried off on a stretcher.<sup id="cite_ref-knee19_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knee19-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He underwent surgery in September 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laporte represented <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> at <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-17_football_team" title="France national under-17 football team">under-17</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-18_football_team" title="France national under-18 football team">under-18</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-19_football_team" title="France national under-19 football team">under-19</a> and <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-21_football_team" title="France national under-21 football team">under-21 levels</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captaining</a> each side.<sup id="cite_ref-Spain_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spain-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He was part of the under-19 team that came runners-up to <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-19_football_team" title="Serbia national under-19 football team">Serbia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2013 UEFA European Championship</a> in Lithuania, and was selected in the Team of the Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-UEFA_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UEFA-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 24 March 2016, in a <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification" title="2017 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification">2017 European Under-21 Championship qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Scotland national under-21 football team">Scotland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Angers" title="Angers">Angers</a>, Laporte was stretchered off with a fracture and dislocation to his right <a href="/wiki/Fibula" title="Fibula">fibula</a> and ankle, concluding his season prematurely.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Previously, in October 2015, he stated that he would consider representing <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> if rejected by France for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Spain_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spain-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2016, at the behest of new national manager <a href="/wiki/Julen_Lopetegui" title="Julen Lopetegui">Julen Lopetegui</a>, Laporte began the process of making himself eligible for Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> A month later, he was called up to the senior France squad for <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2018 World Cup qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in October,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and stated he had decided not to apply for Spanish nationality with the continued aim of playing for France.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He did not take part in either match.
</p><p>In August 2019, he was called up for France's <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020_qualifying_Group_H" title="UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying Group H">Euro 2020 qualifying</a> fixtures against <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andorra_national_football_team" title="Andorra national football team">Andorra</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> however just two days later he was injured playing for his club and ruled out of any involvement.<sup id="cite_ref-knee19_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knee19-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spanish football journalist <a href="/wiki/Guillem_Balagu%C3%A9" title="Guillem Balagué">Guillem Balagué</a> described Laporte as a "centre-back who can play from the back and is strong", adding that "he is certainly a powerful defender".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aymeric_Laporte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
