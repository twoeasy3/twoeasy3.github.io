<p><b>Frenkie de Jong</b> (born 12 May 1997) is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for Spanish club <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>. A versatile <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a>, he is known for his strong defensive abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De Jong began his professional career at <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a> in 2015. He transferred to <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> for a fee worth €1 a year later. The fee was symbolic and offset by a sell-on clause. At Ajax he established himself as one of the best young <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielders</a> in Europe, after winning a domestic double and reaching the Champions League <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League#Semi-finals" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League">semi-finals</a>. De Jong won the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Eredivisie" title="2018–19 Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> and <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_KNVB_Cup" title="2018–19 KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a>, was <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Eredivisie#Annual_awards" title="2018–19 Eredivisie">Eredivisie player of the season</a>, and was an instrumental part of Ajax's first <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> semi-final appearance in <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1996–97 UEFA Champions League">22 years</a>, in a breakthrough <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2018–19 AFC Ajax season">2018–19 season</a>.
</p><p>During his breakthrough season, De Jong agreed to sign for Barcelona in January 2019, leading him to be  signed for the club in July, for a fee worth €75 million. De Jong was selected to the <a href="/wiki/FIFPro#FIFA_FIFPro_Men&#39;s_World11" title="FIFPro">2019 FIFA FIFPro World11</a> and was one of three Dutch players to feature in it for the first time in five years.
</p><p>De Jong made his senior international debut for the Netherlands in September 2018. He became a starter in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Nations_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Nations League">UEFA Nations League</a>, in which Netherlands finished <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Nations_League_Finals" title="2019 UEFA Nations League Finals">runners-up</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Willem_II">Willem II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Willem II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A product of <a href="/wiki/Willem_II_(football_club)" title="Willem II (football club)">Willem II</a>, de Jong played through the entire youth system at the club. On 10 May 2015, De Jong made his <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> debut against <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a>, two days before his eighteenth birthday. He was <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substituted</a> in the 68th minute for <a href="/wiki/Terell_Ondaan" title="Terell Ondaan">Terell Ondaan</a> in a (1–0) home win.<sup id="cite_ref-soccerway2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-soccerway2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the beginning of the season, on 22 August 2015, Ajax purchased De Jong. He signed a four-year contract, for a symbolic €1 fee which included a 10% sell-on bonus. On 23 August 2015, he was loaned back to Willem II until 31 December 2015. During this time he made one substitute appearance in an Eredivisie match against <a href="/wiki/PEC_Zwolle" title="PEC Zwolle">PEC Zwolle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317:_Player_development"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17:_Player_development">2016–17: Player development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–17: Player development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For the first two years at Ajax, De Jong was in and out of the first team squad. After returning to the club in January, 2016, de Jong made 15 appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Eerste_Divisie" title="2015–16 Eerste Divisie">2015—16 Eerste Divisie</a> for <a href="/wiki/Jong_Ajax" title="Jong Ajax">Ajax II</a>. He primarily played in the centre of a three-man midfield and scored two goals. The following year, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Eerste_Divisie" title="2016–17 Eerste Divisie">2016–17</a> Eerste Divisie's Talent of the season award for his performances with <a href="/wiki/Jong_Ajax" title="Jong Ajax">Ajax II</a> for whom, he made 31 first team appearances and played as a central, and defensive, midfielder.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made his senior debut against <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a> coming on, in the 88th minute, for <a href="/wiki/Lasse_Sch%C3%B6ne" title="Lasse Schöne">Lasse Schöne</a>. In total, he made four senior appearances for Ajax, and scored one goal against <a href="/wiki/Go_Ahead_Eagles" title="Go Ahead Eagles">Go Ahead Eagles</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2016–17 AFC Ajax season">2016—17 season</a>. He started one game, came off the bench thrice, and was an unused substitute in 13 games. He also made his European debut and made four substitute, and three bench, appearances, in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2016–17 UEFA Europa League">2016–17 Europa League</a>. He came off the bench in the 82nd minute against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_UEFA_Europa_League_Final" title="2017 UEFA Europa League Final">final</a>. It was Ajax's first European final in 21 years.<sup id="cite_ref-Award_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Award-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9319:_First_team_regular"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–19:_First_team_regular">2017–19: First team regular</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2017–19: First team regular">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Aged 20, he made 26 appearances for the first team. Primarily he featured as a center-back, in a back four, alongside national teammate <a href="/wiki/Matthijs_de_Ligt" title="Matthijs de Ligt">Matthijs de Ligt</a> due to the departure of <a href="/wiki/Davinson_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Davinson Sánchez">Davinson Sanchez</a>. He also played in a three-man center-midfield. He recorded 8 assists in 22 league appearances and received two yellow cards. Towards the end of the season he was injured—tendonitis, syndesmotic ligament tear, hairline crack in calf bone—and missed three months. De Jong made two substitute appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">2017–18 Champions League qualification</a>, as Ajax lost on away goals against <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">Nice</a> (3–3) in the third qualifying <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League#Qualifying_rounds" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">round</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2018–19 AFC Ajax season">2018–19</a> season he primarily played in the middle of a three-man midfield. De Jong was the Eredivisie player of the month in December (2018) and February (2019)—in which he completed 354 of 390 passes and made 53 ball recoveries. Later that month he received praise from <a href="/wiki/Rafael_van_der_Vaart" title="Rafael van der Vaart">Rafael Van Der Vaart</a>, who said De Jong was among the best players in Europe with the ball at his feet. It is unclear whether during or prior to the beginning of the campaign he rejected an offer from Tottenham Hotspur, saying the "moment wasn't right," and wanted to play a full season in his preferred position at Ajax.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De Jong achieved success, and widespread recognition, during the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2018–19 AFC Ajax season">2018–19 season</a> and led Ajax to their first domestic double (league and cup) since the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2001–02 AFC Ajax season">2001–02 season</a>. Ajax wrestled with <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_PSV_Eindhoven_season" title="2018–19 PSV Eindhoven season">PSV Eindhoven</a> all season for the league, and finished the last five weeks at the top of the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_AFC_Ajax_season#Eredivisie_standings_2018–19" title="2018–19 AFC Ajax season">table</a>. He made 51 total appearances and starred in Ajax's first leg (0–1) win over <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League_knockout_phase#Semi-finals" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League knockout phase">2018–19 UEFA Champions League semi-final</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_Stadium" title="Tottenham Hotspur Stadium">Tottenham Hotspur Stadium</a>—in which De Jong had the most touches by any player on the pitch (87) and an 86.2 percent pass completion rate. It was the first time Ajax made the tournament semi-final since <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1996–97 UEFA Champions League">1997</a>. The team eliminated Juventus (3–2 on <a href="/wiki/Playoff_format" title="Playoff format">agg.</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League">quarterfinals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a> (5–3 on agg.) In the second leg of the semi-final, Ajax lost 2–3 at home and were knocked out on <a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">away goals</a>. De Jong was selected to the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League#Squad_of_the_season" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League">2018–19 UEFA Champions League squad of the season</a> and voted <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Champions_League#Midfielder_of_the_season" title="2018–19 UEFA Champions League">the tournament's midfielder of the season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barcelona">Barcelona</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Barcelona">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 January 2019, <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> club <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a> announced the signing of de Jong on a five-year contract, effective from 1 July 2019, for an initial fee worth €75 million. He made his competitive debut on 16 August 2019, in a 0–1 opening day defeat to <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Bilbao</a>. De Jong had transfer discussions with <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> before ultimately opting for Barcelona.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De Jong has primarily played as a central midfielder. In his first 12 league appearances, he had scored and assisted, once each, in the same game—against <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a> on 14 September 2019, at <a href="/wiki/Camp_Nou" title="Camp Nou">Camp Nou</a>. His first assist was to <a href="/wiki/Ansu_Fati" title="Ansu Fati">Ansu Fati</a> in the 2nd minute. He set up Fati with a low cross from the right. Fati then assisted for de Jong's first goal, in the 7th minute. Fati cut a low cross in from the left which de Jong scored from just outside the <a href="/wiki/Six_yard_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Six yard box">six-yard box</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During his youth career, de Jong made 22 appearances in the Netherlands youth setup. On 10 July 2015, he made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-19_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-19 football team">Netherlands U19</a> team against Germany U19. On 6 September 2018, de Jong replaced <a href="/wiki/Georginio_Wijnaldum" title="Georginio Wijnaldum">Georginio Wijnaldum</a> at half-time to make his senior international debut in a friendly (2–1) win against <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a>. Fifteen minutes into his debut, he assisted a goal for <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Depay" title="Memphis Depay">Memphis Depay</a> by retrieving possession high-up the pitch and setting up the forward. He later on scored the winner, and had a 100 percent pass completion rate (21 passes) in the opposition half. He soon became a regular starter for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Nations_League_A" title="2018–19 UEFA Nations League A">2018–19 UEFA Nations League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2020_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2020 qualifiers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%; ;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1012376600">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:640px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{width:100%!important;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"With his talent, at his age, he could become a <a href="/wiki/Xavi" title="Xavi">Xavi</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Andres_Iniesta" class="mw-redirect" title="Andres Iniesta">Iniesta</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Overmars_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Overmars-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Former Netherlands international and <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> sporting director <a href="/wiki/Marc_Overmars" title="Marc Overmars">Marc Overmars</a> on De Jong</cite>
</p>
</div><p>Frenkie has described himself as a player "who likes to have the ball a lot, and play possession." A versatile player, de Jong can function as a <a href="/wiki/Playmaker#Deep-lying_playmakers" title="Playmaker">regista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Central midfielder">central midfielder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holding_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Holding midfielder">holding midfielder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Box-to-box_midfielder" class="mw-redirect" title="Box-to-box midfielder">box-to-box midfielder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Centre_back" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre back">centre back</a>. He has been lauded for his combination of defensive stability and playmaking ability. He often partnered <a href="/wiki/Matthijs_de_Ligt" title="Matthijs de Ligt">Matthijs de Ligt</a> at centre-back, at Ajax. As a centre back, he abstained from committing fouls, showed great positional sense, and won possession frequently. In this more defensive role, his performances led to comparison with former German <a href="/wiki/Sweeper_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sweeper (association football)">sweeper</a> <a href="/wiki/Franz_Beckenbauer" title="Franz Beckenbauer">Franz Beckenbauer</a> and likened his tendency to progress forward in possession, and ability to play-make, from defense to the German legend.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Overmars_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Overmars-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sensation_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sensation-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></p><div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%; ;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1012376600"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"We saw the other day De Jong is a footballer who can mark an era in Barcelona. He is able to dominate the game, he sees it easily and doesn't lose the ball. He also makes sacrifices to work defensively. I think they are top-level players, very good for Barca."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Former Barcelona midfielder Xavi on De Jong<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div><p>De Jong's vision has been praised alongside his ability to <a href="/wiki/Dribbling#Association_football" title="Dribbling">dribble</a> in narrow spaces, control over possession, and decisive passing. A natural passer, de Jong had a 91.4 percent pass accuracy, in all competitions, during his last season with Ajax. An extraordinary game-reader, he often makes offensive runs through the middle.
</p><p>His other attributes include a penchant for long cross-field passes, creating space, and absorbing attacking pressure. His close control, accuracy, work rate, and movement has drawn comparisons with <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Iniesta" title="Andrés Iniesta">Andrés Iniesta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johan_Cruyff" title="Johan Cruyff">Johan Cruyff</a>. At Barcelona, he has primarily played on either side of a three-man central midfield. This has led experts and former players regard him as a player with "Barça DNA" who was "born to play for Barcelona."<sup id="cite_ref-sensation_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sensation-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frenkie_de_Jong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De Jong was born in <a href="/wiki/Gorinchem" title="Gorinchem">Gorinchem</a> and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Arkel" title="Arkel">Arkel</a>, a town in the province of South Holland, where he started playing football at a young age. Since he started playing professionally, De Jong has chosen 21 on his shirt as a tribute to his grandfather who was born on 21 April.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
He met his girlfriend Mikky Kiemeny in high school and they have been dating since 2014.
</p>
