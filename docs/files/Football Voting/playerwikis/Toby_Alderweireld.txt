<p><b>Toby Albertine Maurits Alderweireld</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[tobi ldrrlt]</a></span>;<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> born 2 March 1989) is a Belgian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium national team</a>. Mainly a <a href="/wiki/Centre_back" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre back">centre back</a>, he can also play as a <a href="/wiki/Right_back_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Right back (association football)">right back</a>.
</p><p>Alderweireld began his professional career at Dutch club <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, where he won honours including three consecutive <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> titles. In 2013, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, where he won <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> and reached the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> final in his first season. After a year on loan at <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>, he moved to Tottenham Hotspur in 2015 and once again reached the Champions League final in <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2019 UEFA Champions League Final">2019</a>.
</p><p>A full international since 2009, Alderweireld has earned over 100 caps for Belgium and represented the country at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Alderweireld moved to <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>'s <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax_Youth_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="AFC Ajax Youth Academy">youth academy</a> in August 2004 from <a href="/wiki/K.F.C._Germinal_Beerschot" class="mw-redirect" title="K.F.C. Germinal Beerschot">Germinal Beerschot</a>. On 22 February 2007, he signed his first professional contract, tying him to the club until 30 June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was promoted to the first team for the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 season</a>, but only made his first team debut on 18 January, in the 4–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/N.E.C._(football_club)" class="mw-redirect" title="N.E.C. (football club)">NEC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On 26 February, he made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">European</a> debut during a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Although he only made several appearances for the remainder of the season, he was awarded a new long-term contract extension until 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TobyAlderweireld.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/TobyAlderweireld.jpg/170px-TobyAlderweireld.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="295" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/TobyAlderweireld.jpg/255px-TobyAlderweireld.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/TobyAlderweireld.jpg/340px-TobyAlderweireld.jpg 2x" data-file-width="343" data-file-height="595" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TobyAlderweireld.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alderweireld with Ajax.</div></div></div>
<p>During the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Eredivisie" title="2009–10 Eredivisie">2009–10 season</a>, Alderweireld established himself in the first team and starting XI. He became first-choice centre back along with fellow Belgian <a href="/wiki/Jan_Vertonghen" title="Jan Vertonghen">Jan Vertonghen</a> following the departure of former captain <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Vermaelen" title="Thomas Vermaelen">Thomas Vermaelen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> New Ajax coach <a href="/wiki/Martin_Jol" title="Martin Jol">Martin Jol</a> praised the two defenders and kept faith in the duo after a string of good performances.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> During the 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Heracles_Almelo" title="Heracles Almelo">Heracles</a> on 4 September, he scored his first league goal for the club when he opened the scoring with a header from <a href="/wiki/Luis_Su%C3%A1rez" title="Luis Suárez">Luis Suárez</a>'s corner-kick.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On 27 January, he scored a late equaliser in extra time during the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_KNVB_Cup" title="2009–10 KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_KNVB_Cup#Quarter_finals" title="2009–10 KNVB Cup">quarter-final</a> against NEC; <a href="/wiki/Siem_de_Jong" title="Siem de Jong">Siem de Jong</a> scored five minutes before the final whistle to steal a 3–2 win for Ajax. At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Eredivisie" title="2009–10 Eredivisie">2009–10 season</a>, Alderweireld was named Ajax Talent of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He assured himself of a place in the Eredivisie giants' history books on 3 November 2011, heading in Ajax's 100th UEFA Champions League goal in the 2–1 Group G defeat by <a href="/wiki/AJ_Auxerre" title="AJ Auxerre">Auxerre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Eredivisie" title="2010–11 Eredivisie">2010–11 season</a>, he scored a stunning 25-yard strike at the <a href="/wiki/San_Siro" title="San Siro">San Siro</a> against <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> group rivals <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> in a 2–0 away win. He followed this spectacular goal with an even more impressive 32-yard strike against <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> in a 2–0 home win.
In the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2011–12 AFC Ajax season">2011–12 season</a>, Alderweireld made significant strides in his ability as a footballer, also forming a formidable partnership in the heart of the Ajax defensive with captain and fellow Belgian Jan Vertonghen. Alderweireld also showed some impressive offensive ability, scoring a remarkable goal against <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">Heerenveen</a>.
</p><p>On 5 August 2012, during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Johan_Cruyff_Shield" title="2012 Johan Cruyff Shield">Johan Cruyff Shield</a> encounter against <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>, Alderweireld managed to score for Ajax in the 44th minute, bringing his club back within a goals difference going into halftime 2–1. Unfortunately, it was a match Ajax would lose 4–2, making it the third Johan Cruyff Shield which Alderweireld participated in and finished as runners-up. The following <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_AFC_Ajax_season" title="2012–13 AFC Ajax season">2012–13 season</a>, however, would be a successful one, marking the third year in a row that Alderweireld would play Champions League football, and also winning his third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> title for Ajax and 32nd overall, having played as a starting centre back for Ajax in all three championship pursuits. Alderweireld scored a further three goals that season for Ajax, including two goals during the regular season against <a href="/wiki/PEC_Zwolle" title="PEC Zwolle">PEC Zwolle</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">Twente</a>, as well as scoring another goal in a 2–0 home victory against <a href="/wiki/FC_Steaua_Bucure%C8%99ti" class="mw-redirect" title="FC Steaua Bucureti">Steaua Bucureti</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Amsterdam Arena">Amsterdam Arena</a>, in the first leg of the Round of 32 in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Europa_League_knockout_phase#First_leg" title="2012–13 UEFA Europa League knockout phase">2012–13 UEFA Europa League</a>. Having been drawn into a group with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> and eventual runners-up <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> during its Champions League campaign, Ajax had entered into the Europa League at the knockout phase having secured third place in their group, but failed to advance, losing in the second leg match in Romania 4–2 on penalty kicks after extra time.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg/220px-Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="174" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg/330px-Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg/440px-Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2307" data-file-height="1828" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Basis_Elftal_Ajax_14Sep2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alderweireld with Ajax teammates in 2011.</div></div></div>
<p>With one more year remaining on his contract, Alderweireld opted not to extend his contract with Ajax, subsequently receiving plenty of interest during the off-season, with <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> being mentioned as the most keen to sign the young defender.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlético_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 September 2013, Alderweireld joined <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> on a four-year contract for an estimated €7&#160;million (£6.16&#160;million) transfer fee.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He played 12 matches as the club won <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>; on 11 May, he headed his only goal of the season, an equaliser in a 1–1 home draw with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga</a> in the penultimate round of fixtures, ensuring that Atlético remained in the title race.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Alderweireld played the final seven minutes and extra time of the <a href="/wiki/2014_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2014 UEFA Champions League Final">2014 Champions League final</a> for Atlético in place of <a href="/wiki/Filipe_Lu%C3%ADs" title="Filipe Luís">Filipe Luís</a>; the team was beating <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Derby" title="Madrid Derby">city rival</a> Real Madrid 1–0 when he entered the field of play, but Atlético eventually lost 1–4.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Southampton_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Southampton_(loan)">Southampton (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Southampton (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Alderweireld was sent on a season-long loan to English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> on 1 September 2014, with the option for Southampton to buy him at the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He made his Southampton debut on 13 September, helping to keep a clean sheet in a 4–0 victory against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On 26 December, he scored his first goal for the club, Southampton's third in a 3–1 away win away to <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> by heading <a href="/wiki/James_Ward-Prowse" title="James Ward-Prowse">James Ward-Prowse</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Corner_kick" title="Corner kick">corner kick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Southampton had a £6.8&#160;million option to buy Alderweireld, but in July 2015 Atlético cancelled the clause for £1.5&#160;million in order to sell to the highest bidder. Subsequent reports claimed that Atlético failed to exercise their buy-out in time.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tottenham Hotspur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 July 2015, Alderweireld joined <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> on a five-year contract which runs until the summer of 2020 at <a href="/wiki/White_Hart_Lane" title="White Hart Lane">White Hart Lane</a>, despite Southampton threatening legal action over the transfer.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> At Spurs, he was reunited with former Ajax teammates <a href="/wiki/Christian_Eriksen" title="Christian Eriksen">Christian Eriksen</a> and Jan Vertonghen, who previously joined the north London side from Ajax.  On 30 July, Alderweireld made his club debut in the <a href="/wiki/2015_MLS_All-Star_Game" title="2015 MLS All-Star Game">2015 MLS All-Star Game</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>. During a 2–1 defeat, Alderweireld started the game and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Federico_Fazio" title="Federico Fazio">Federico Fazio</a> after 62 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2015.E2.80.9316_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–16_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 26 September, Alderweireld scored his first goal for Tottenham in a 4–1 win over Manchester City at White Hart Lane.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> His second goal for the club came in another 4–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/London_derby" class="mw-redirect" title="London derby">London rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 22 November.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On 5 March 2016, in the 183rd <a href="/wiki/North_London_Derby" class="mw-redirect" title="North London Derby">North London Derby</a>, Alderweireld scored for Tottenham in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A month later, he was Spurs' second scorer in a 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his first season at Tottenham, Alderweireld has been recognised by commentators as one of the best defenders in the Premier League,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> forming impressive central defensive partnerships with national team colleague Jan Vertonghen,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and later the Austrian <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Wimmer" title="Kevin Wimmer">Kevin Wimmer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> This form helped Tottenham to achieve the best defensive record in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Premier_League" title="2015–16 Premier League">Premier League</a>, with only 25 goals conceded from 34 games.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Over the season the club had the joint-lowest number of goals conceded (35) in the Premier League.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_2016_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian_2016-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He was chosen as a centre back for the <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year" title="PFA Team of the Year">PFA Team of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C._season" title="2016–17 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season">2016–17 season</a>, he was a regular in the Spurs starting line-up until an injury sustained at <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> on 15 October.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> This injury kept him on the injury list for several weeks,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> until he made his return in the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2016–17 UEFA Champions League group stage">Champions League</a> as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> on 7 December.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Alderweireld picked up the final goal of the season for Tottenham, coming in the 7–1 win over already-relegated <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> on 21 May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He was part of the Tottenham defense that achieved the best defensive record in the club's history, with only 26 goals conceded in the Premier League this season (only 9 of which were conceded in home league games), 6 better than the previous record of 32 conceded that was set in the <a href="/wiki/1908%E2%80%9309_in_English_football" title="1908–09 in English football">1908–09 season</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9318_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–18_season">2017–18 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017–18 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Alderweireld played in all the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Premier_League" title="2017–18 Premier League">Premier League</a> and <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> games from the start of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C._season" title="2017–18 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season">2017–18 season</a>, but on 1 November 2017, he suffered a <a href="/wiki/Hamstring" title="Hamstring">hamstring</a> injury in the home game against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a> that ended in a 3–1 win.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The injury would keep him out of the team for a few months.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On 7 February 2018, he returned to the starting lineup in the fourth round replay of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_FA_Cup" title="2017–18 FA Cup">FA Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Newport_County_A.F.C." title="Newport County A.F.C.">Newport County</a>, which Tottenham won 2–0.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> However, an injury to the tendon of his hamstring suffered while training two weeks later meant that he was again out of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He returned to the squad in April towards the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2018.E2.80.9319_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–19_season">2018–19 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2018–19 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After a season curtailed by injuries and being out of favour, Alderweireld again became a regular player for Tottenham in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C._season" title="2018–19 Tottenham Hotspur F.C. season">2018–19 season</a>.  In January 2019, Tottenham took up an option to extend his contract until 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> He scored an own goal in the last minute in the tightly fought game against Liverpool, losing 2–1.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2019.E2.80.9320_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019–20_season">2019–20 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2019–20 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 20 December 2019, Alderweireld signed a contract extension to 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> On 16 February 2020, Alderweireld scored his first goal for the club since May 2017, making amends for an earlier own goal, and helped Tottenham win 3–2 away to <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> On 12 July 2020, he scored an 81st-minute winner against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in a <a href="/wiki/North_London_derby" title="North London derby">North London derby</a> to help his team to a 2–1 victory.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2020.E2.80.9321_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020–21_season">2020–21 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2020–21 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 2 January 2021, Alderweireld scored the first goal of the 2020–21 season in a 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg/170px-Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg/255px-Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg/340px-Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="666" data-file-height="1000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Toby_Alderweireld_2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alderweireld with <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a></div></div></div>
<p>Alderweireld first represented Belgium at <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_youth_football_team" title="Belgium national youth football team">under-17 level</a> in October 2005 and took part in the <a href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2006 UEFA U17 Championship</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 season</a> concluded, he made his senior debut for <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a> in a friendly tournament, <a href="/wiki/Kirin_Cup" title="Kirin Cup">Kirin Cup</a>, against <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> on 29 May 2009. However, his full senior debut came three months later in a match against the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_football_team" title="Czech Republic national football team">Czech Republic</a>. He was named in several <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2010 World Cup qualifying</a> squads, but was mostly an unused substitute. In October 2009, he came on as a second-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Van_Buyten" title="Daniel Van Buyten">Daniel Van Buyten</a> in the 2–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a>.
</p><p>Alderweireld was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_under-21_football_team" title="Belgium national under-21 football team">Belgian U21 squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">2011 UEFA U21 Championship qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-21_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-21 football team">Ukraine</a> on 13 November 2009, with the match finishing 2–0 to Ukraine. He played <a href="/wiki/Right_back_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Right back (association football)">right back</a> during the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">2012 UEFA Euro qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_football_team" title="Turkey national football team">Turkey</a> on 7 September 2010.
</p><p>On 13 May 2014, Alderweireld was named in the squad to go to the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> He started in all five games of Belgium's tournament run, including its 1–0 defeat against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> in the quarter-finals.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>, he won a bronze medal with Belgium after a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> On 8 September 2020, Alderweireld played his 100th match for Belgium in a 5–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Iceland_national_football_team" title="Iceland national football team">Iceland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toby_Alderweireld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alderweireld married Shani Van Mieghem in <a href="/wiki/Ekeren" title="Ekeren">Ekeren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a> on 19 June 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> They had their first child Ayla born in September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> His son Jace was born in February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
